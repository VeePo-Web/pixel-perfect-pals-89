# Research 01 — Local & Maps Ranking for "Areas We Serve" Pages

> **Stream:** How Google ranks local/Maps results and service-area pages — and how to rank #1 and capture the most traffic.
> **Compiled:** 2026-06 · Fresh web research, cross-checked across ≥2 sources per claim. Primary sources cited inline.
> **Status:** Evidence dossier. Feeds → `reports/00-MASTER-STRATEGY.md` and `reports/02-NATIONAL-SCALE-TEMPLATE-SPEC.md`.

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY — 10 highest-leverage, evidence-backed tactics

1. **Nail the GBP primary category before anything else.** It is the single strongest relevance signal. "If your primary category is 'General Contractor' instead of 'Plumber,' you're not even in the running" for that search, regardless of reviews ([Drawbridge 2025-26](https://www.drawbridgemarketing.com/blog/google-business-profile-categories-complete-guide/)). Use 3–5 accurate categories; irrelevant ones *dilute* relevance.

2. **Drive sustained review velocity, not a one-time pile.** Sterling Sky's 8,186-business study found *"the number of reviews you've gotten this month matters more than your total"* — and rankings can *"fall off a cliff if you stop receiving reviews for even three weeks"* ([Sterling Sky 2025](https://www.sterlingsky.ca/what-gets-you-ranking-for-near-me-2025/)). Target ≥4 new reviews/month, ongoing.

3. **Earn text-based, keyword-rich reviews.** *"Reviews with actual text had a stronger impact on rankings"* than star-only ratings; Google parses the words for relevance ([Sterling Sky](https://www.sterlingsky.ca/what-gets-you-ranking-for-near-me-2025/)). These also feed local "justification" snippets.

4. **Hit the "magic 10" review threshold, then keep the cadence.** A measurable Maps bump appears moving 9→10 reviews; the count-based jump plateaus after, so velocity/recency is where to invest beyond that ([Sterling Sky review-count study](https://www.sterlingsky.ca/number-of-reviews-impact-ranking/)).

5. **Make every "Areas We Serve" page genuinely local (≈40–60% unique content).** Google's spam policy explicitly lists *"blocks of text that list cities and regions a page is trying to rank for"* as keyword stuffing, and **doorway abuse** covers *"pages targeted at specific regions or cities that funnel users to one page"* ([Google Spam Policies](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials/spam-policies)). Defensible local value: landmarks, local regulations, seasonal conditions, named projects, technician bios, local testimonials ([Sterling Sky](https://www.sterlingsky.ca/how-to-create-unique-and-helpful-service-area-pages-for-local-businesses/)).

6. **Substantive landing-page content correlates with ranking.** Pages with *more meaningful words* ranked better (keyword-stuffing excluded) — thin city pages lose. On-page is the #1 organic factor (~33%) and a top-3 local-pack factor (~15%) ([BrightLocal 2026](https://www.brightlocal.com/learn/google-local-algorithm-and-ranking-factors/)).

7. **Understand the SAB address paradox and decide deliberately.** Google says service-area businesses *should* hide their address, but Sterling Sky's controlled tests showed hiding caused *"a massive drop in calls"* and the local pack *"vanished entirely"* for some keywords, reversing when re-added ([Sterling Sky](https://www.sterlingsky.ca/hiding-address-google-business-ranking/)). A visible address positively correlated with ranking in the 8,186-business study.

8. **Proximity matters less than its reputation for non-"near me" queries — and you can't directly pull it.** One 2025 estimate pegs it ~15% for general queries; Whitespark's expert panel still ranks it #1 (55%) — they reconcile by query type (proximity dominates "near me"/ungeo-modified) ([Whitespark 2026](https://whitespark.ca/local-search-ranking-factors/); [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/learn/google-local-algorithm-and-ranking-factors/)).

9. **Optimize separately for the map pack vs. the blue links — they're different algorithms.** Pack = relevance/proximity/prominence + GBP (~32%) + reviews (~20%). Organic = on-page (~33%) + links (~24%) ([BrightLocal 2026](https://www.brightlocal.com/learn/google-local-algorithm-and-ranking-factors/)).

10. **Treat 2026 AI surfaces as the same job with reweighted inputs.** Local + AI signals have *"effectively merged"* — for AI visibility, GBP *drops to ~12%*, on-page *rises to ~24%*, citations/links each ~13% ([BrightLocal/Whitespark 2026](https://www.brightlocal.com/learn/google-local-algorithm-and-ranking-factors/)). Structured, verified, matching data (GBP + site + schema) is what LLMs require before recommending you.

## 1. The three pillars — current weighting & what moves them

Google's official position: *"Local results are based primarily on relevance, distance, and prominence"* ([Google Business Profile Help](https://support.google.com/business/answer/7091?hl=en)).

- **Relevance** — how well the profile matches the query. **Almost entirely in your control.** Primary category is the dominant lever; profile completeness, services, and matching website content reinforce it. Est. ~25% of the mix ([Colling Media 2025](https://collingmedia.com/search-engine-optimization-seo/google-my-business-local-ranking-factors/)).
- **Distance/proximity** — how far the business is from the searcher. **Most over-discussed.** ~15% (down from 25–30% in 2020) for general queries vs. Whitespark's expert call of **55%**. *Sources disagree*; reconcile by query type: proximity dominates "near me"/un-modified searches and recedes for explicit-location queries.
- **Prominence** — how well-known: links, **review count/score**, citations, web authority. 2026 shift: Google moving from pure "prominence" toward **"popularity"/engagement** — real clicks, calls, direction requests, review cadence — letting a high-engagement newer business outrank an older one ([Whitespark 2026](https://whitespark.ca/local-search-ranking-factors/); [Digital Applied 2026](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/local-seo-core-updates-gbp-strategy-may-2026)).

Google is explicit: *"There's no way to request or pay for a better local ranking."*

## 2. Google Business Profile — the engine of local/Maps visibility

GBP is the heaviest local-pack factor: ~**32%** of pack weight ([BrightLocal 2026](https://www.brightlocal.com/learn/google-local-algorithm-and-ranking-factors/)).

- **Categories:** 1 primary + up to 9 secondary (10 total). Use **3–5 accurate** ones. Primary = strongest relevance signal; changing it may trigger re-verification ([Accentuate 2025](https://www.accentuate.agency/2025-google-business-profile-categories/)).
- **Service areas (SAB):** Specify by city/postal code/area — **no radius**, max **20 areas**, overall footprint *"shouldn't extend farther than about 2 hours' driving time"* from base ([Google Help](https://support.google.com/business/answer/9157481?hl=en)).
- **Show vs. hide address — the paradox:** Google tells home-based SABs to clear the address; Sterling Sky documents ranking declines, call collapse, and pack disappearance when hidden — reversing on restoration ([Sterling Sky](https://www.sterlingsky.ca/hiding-address-google-business-ranking/)). Decide per-business with eyes open.
- **2023–2026 enforcement:** tougher **video verification**; a 2025–26 crackdown on **keyword-stuffed business names** causing suspensions ([Digital Applied 2026](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/local-seo-core-updates-gbp-strategy-may-2026)).
- **Attributes, products/services, Q&A, posts:** completeness/engagement signals rising in weight. Photos correlate with ranking in *visual* industries but show *minimal* impact for service trades ([Sterling Sky 2025](https://www.sterlingsky.ca/what-gets-you-ranking-for-near-me-2025/)).

## 3. Review signals — count vs velocity vs recency vs keywords

The clearest evidence base of all:

- **Count:** ~**20%** of pack weight. Real but small bump at **9→10 reviews**, then diminishing returns ([Sterling Sky count study](https://www.sterlingsky.ca/number-of-reviews-impact-ranking/)).
- **Velocity & recency (headline finding):** *"The number of reviews you've gotten this month matters more than your total"* — stopping ~3 weeks can collapse rankings. Consumer-side: **74% want reviews from the last 3 months**; 31% will only use 4.5★+ businesses ([Sterling Sky 2025](https://www.sterlingsky.ca/what-gets-you-ranking-for-near-me-2025/); BrightLocal LCRS 2026).
- **Keywords-in-reviews:** text reviews outweigh star-only; Google parses words for relevance + justification snippets.
- **Responding:** Whitespark's 2026 survey tracks **owner response within 48 hours** as a behavioral signal.

---

## 4. "Areas We Serve" pages — ranking vs. getting filtered

These feed **both** local-pack relevance (matching landing content) and classic organic (on-page ~33% of organic weight).

**What gets them filtered (Google policy):**
- **Doorway abuse:** *"pages targeted at specific regions or cities that funnel users to one page."*
- **Scaled content abuse:** *"many pages generated for the primary purpose of manipulating rankings and not helping users."* The **Aug 26 2025 spam update** targeted scaled/thin content (after March & June 2025 core updates).
- **Keyword stuffing:** *"blocks of text that list cities and regions."*

**What makes them rank:** roughly **40–60% genuinely unique, location-specific content** per page ([HireAWriter 2025](https://www.hireawriter.us/seo/how-to-optimize-service-area-pages-for-local-search-rankings)). Defensible value: local landmarks/neighborhoods, municipal regulations/permits, seasonal/climate conditions, named local projects, local testimonials, technician bios, realistic scheduling windows. **Prioritize depth over coverage** — build rich pages for top cities first; expand only when you have something genuinely useful to say ([Sterling Sky](https://www.sterlingsky.ca/how-to-create-unique-and-helpful-service-area-pages-for-local-businesses/)).

---

## 5. Proximity / "near me" / address-visibility studies

Sterling Sky's **8,186 businesses across 200 cities** study (top-10 pack results) is the definitive 2025 evidence ([Sterling Sky](https://www.sterlingsky.ca/what-gets-you-ranking-for-near-me-2025/)):
- **Positive correlations:** visible business address, higher review rating, substantive landing-page content, **recent review volume**, **text-based reviews**, industry-relevant photos.
- **Negative correlation:** *being a service-area business / hiding the address* — tensioned against Google's own SAB guidance.
- Proximity dominates "near me"/un-geo-modified queries; for explicit "[service] in [city]" it's balanced against relevance/prominence ([Search Engine Land proximity guide](https://searchengineland.com/guide/google-proximity-bias-in-local-search)).

---

## 6. Local pack vs. local organic — and justifications

- **Local pack (3-pack):** map thumbnail, stars, click-to-call, directions; relevance + proximity + prominence, GBP (~32%), reviews (~20%).
- **Local organic (blue links):** on-page (~33%), links (~24%), behavioral (~10%).
- **Local Finder:** expanded list behind "More places."
- **Justifications/snippets:** Google pulls quotes *from your reviews* and *from your website/GBP content* matching the query — boosts CTR; win them by having the matching language in reviews + on-page ([Search Engine Land Local Pack guide](https://searchengineland.com/guide/google-local-pack)).

---

## 7. Internal linking, NAP consistency & citations

- **NAP consistency** remains foundational — *"3 of the top 6 foundational ranking factors relate to citation quantity, quality, and consistency."* Necessary-but-not-sufficient. Schema NAP must match GBP + footer **character-for-character**.
- **Citations:** ~**6–7%** direct weight, but disproportionately important as the verification layer LLMs + Google cross-reference.
- **Internal linking:** authority-distribution pyramid — homepage/service pages → city hubs → neighborhood spokes; spokes breadcrumb up. Descriptive anchors only; no orphans.

---

## 8. 2026-specific: AI features & new signals

- **AI Overviews appear in ~40–68% of local queries** (Whitespark avg. 68%); AI local packs show **1–2 businesses** and cover only ~**32%** of a legacy 3-pack ([connectionmodel 2026](https://www.connectionmodel.com/blog/2026-local-seo-the-checklist-to-stay-visible-in-ai-driven-search)).
- **Reweighting for AI visibility:** GBP **↓12%**, on-page **↑24%**, citations & links each **13%**, reviews **16%** ([BrightLocal 2026](https://www.brightlocal.com/learn/google-local-algorithm-and-ranking-factors/)).
- **Merged signals:** the same data feeds Google, Maps, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Apple. **LLMs require verified, matching data + schema** before recommending you ([Whitespark 2026](https://whitespark.ca/local-search-ranking-factors/)).
- **Engagement ascendant:** posts, photos, clicks, calls, direction requests, review cadence keep climbing.
- **Enforcement:** continued GBP suspensions for name keyword-stuffing; Aug 2025 spam update + 2025 core updates hit scaled/thin location pages hardest.

**Where sources disagree:** (a) Proximity weight — ~15% vs. 55% (reconcile by query type). (b) The SAB address question — Google's guidance (hide) vs. Sterling Sky's evidence (showing helps). (c) Exact factor percentages vary by methodology (data-driven vs. expert survey) — read as directional.
