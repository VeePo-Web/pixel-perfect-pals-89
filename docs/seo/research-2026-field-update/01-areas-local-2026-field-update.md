# Field Update 01 — Areas-We-Serve, Local Organic & the Local Pack (2026-06)

> Extends `areas-maps/research/01`–`02` and `AREAS-MAPS-MASTERPLAN.md §1`. Verifies the doorway/uniqueness
> line and the local-pack weights with current sources, and adds the 2026 shifts those briefs predate.

## 0. The one thing that decides everything

Two ranking systems, never to be conflated:

- **Localized organic** — the blue-link results your service-area *pages* can rank in. **This is the only
  surface your pages move.**
- **The Local Pack / Maps** — driven almost entirely by **Google Business Profile** + proximity + reviews.
  **Your pages barely touch it.** [confirmed]

A service-area page's job is to win localized organic + feed AI citation + convert. It is **not** a lever for
the pack. Stop expecting city pages to move Maps rankings — that's a GBP + reviews + proximity job.
→ Sterling Sky, *Does the service area in GBP impact ranking?* (2025-03-31) https://www.sterlingsky.ca/does-the-service-area-in-google-my-business-impact-ranking/

## 1. The doorway / scaled-content line — verified verbatim

Google's policy text, current:

- **Doorway abuse:** *"sites or pages created to rank for specific, similar search queries"* that *"lead users
  to intermediate pages that are not as useful as the final destination,"* with the explicit example: *"multiple
  domain names or pages targeted at specific regions or cities that funnel users to one page."* [confirmed]
- **Scaled content abuse:** *"many pages generated for the primary purpose of manipulating search rankings and
  not helping users… large amounts of unoriginal content that provides little to no value… **no matter how it's
  created**"* (the last clause explicitly captures AI-spun city pages). [confirmed]
  → https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials/spam-policies

The **load-bearing nuance** (this is what makes the template viable): **duplicate/similar content can still rank.**

- Sterling Sky case study: **35 service-area pages at 84% average content similarity all ranked and converted.**
  Google's quoted position: *"Duplicate content on a site is not grounds for action… unless… the intent… is to
  be deceptive and manipulate."* The rule: **similar content is fine when pages target *different geographies*;
  it fails when pages compete for the same query in the same place** — and it's "not always effective in
  competitive markets." [confirmed]
  → https://www.sterlingsky.ca/service-area-pages-duplicate-content/
- **Scale threshold:** safe in the "few dozen" range; *"once you get up into the 100's and 1000's of pages, you
  increase the odds of Google taking notice and giving out a penalty."* A documented manual penalty exists for
  thousands of similar pages. [confirmed]
  → https://www.sterlingsky.ca/stop-1000-hours-on-service-area-pages/
- **Enforcement hardened:** the **Aug 26, 2025 spam update** (27 days — longest in recent memory) explicitly hit
  *"doorway pages, spun content, and low-effort AI articles,"* and templated city pages lost visibility. Bulk
  AI-generated city pages are materially riskier than in 2022–2023. [directional]
  → https://seosherpa.com/googles-august-2025-spam-update/

**Template consequence:** the build-time **4-of-8 uniqueness gate** is not optional polish — it is the
difference between an asset and a domain-wide liability. Generate a page **only** where you can prove ≥4 real
local signals **and** at least 1 first-party element. The find-and-replace test (remove the town name — is
anything location-specific left?) is the publish veto.

## 2. The local-specificity signals that actually move pages, ranked

From Sterling Sky's "unique & helpful service-area pages" work, ordered by emphasis: [directional]

1. **First-party / on-the-job data** — your *own* stats gathered doing the work in that area (cost ranges,
   settlement amounts, response times, job counts). **Highest impact.**
2. **Named-staff expertise** — practitioner bios, certifications, direct quotes.
3. **City-specific reviews / testimonials** — video, before/after, case studies tied to that town.
4. **Job photos from that area.**
5. **Local connections** — named landmarks/neighborhoods *tied to service delivery* (not decoration),
   Chamber/charity involvement, employee community ties.
6. **A local-only FAQ** — a question only that town would ask (permit, code, local condition).

Documented result of doing this well: Minars Dermatology **35 → 1,350 daily clicks; 670 → 1,920 monthly leads.**
→ https://www.sterlingsky.ca/how-to-create-unique-and-helpful-service-area-pages-for-local-businesses/

> Note this **supersedes "word count" thinking** (and aligns with `DEEPDIVE-5 §3.2`): 84% similar pages rank
> because the unique 16% is *real local substance*, not city-name swaps. Length is irrelevant; local substance is the lever.

## 3. Local-Pack ranking weights — Whitespark **2026** (released 2025-11-06)

| Factor group | Local Pack weight |
|---|---|
| Google Business Profile signals | **~32%** |
| On-page signals | ~19% |
| Review signals | ~16% |
| Link signals | ~15% |
| Behavioral signals | ~8% |
| Citation signals | ~7% |
| Personalization | ~3% |

**8 of the top 10 Local-Pack factors come from GBP.** Top individual factors: **primary GBP category** (the
single biggest), **proximity of the verified address to the searcher**, **keywords in the GBP business title.**
[directional] → https://whitespark.ca/local-search-ranking-factors/ · summary https://www.brightlocal.com/learn/google-local-algorithm-and-ranking-factors/

**The new column — "AI Search Visibility" is scored for the first time in 2026.** In that column the weighting
inverts: **on-page is heaviest (~24%) and GBP drops to ~12%.** Translation: **your *pages and content* matter
more than your GBP for getting cited by ChatGPT/Gemini/Perplexity.** This is the single most important
strategic addition for the template — the same city pages that win localized organic are what earn local AI
citations. [directional] → https://harmo.me/en/blog/local-seo-ranking-factors-2026-whitespark

Whitespark 2026 also ranks **"Dedicated Page for Each Service"** #1 in Localized Organic (and #2 in AI
Visibility) — i.e. the service × location matrix is the correct architecture. [directional]

## 4. The 2025 "near me" study — what actually correlated (8,186 businesses / 200 cities, 2025-11-05)

[directional, but unusually large sample] → https://www.sterlingsky.ca/what-gets-you-ranking-for-near-me-2025/

- **Showing a physical address beat hiding it** (hiding correlated *negatively* with ranking) — directly
  contradicts Google's own "hide your address for SABs" advice. **For SABs with a real address, show it.**
- **Review recency/velocity outweighs total count** — a business adding **60+ reviews/month dominated; an
  18-day pause caused a significant ranking decline.** Velocity is a continuous obligation, not a milestone.
- **Text reviews beat star-only** (Google reads review text for categorization).
- **Substantive landing-page vocabulary** correlated with better local rankings; **keyword stuffing did not.**
- **Photos** helped restaurants/salons but had **minimal impact for service trades.**
- Supporting stat: businesses with **50+ Google reviews are 266% more likely to appear in the Local Pack** than
  those with <10. → https://biziq.com/blog/local-seo-statistics/

## 5. Maps embeds & schema — what helps, what doesn't

- A **Google Map embed is not a ranking factor**; Google ended mass embed-per-page credit in **June 2018**. Its
  value is UX + an entity-consistency signal (site ↔ GBP same place). Embed on contact/hub, **not** a unique map
  on hundreds of city pages. [confirmed] → https://norsumediagroup.com/do-google-map-embeds-help-seo-and-improve-local-visibility/
- **`areaServed` supersedes the deprecated `serviceArea`** property. Schema **creates no ranking boost by
  itself** and *"will not compensate for thin/duplicate location pages or weak local signals"* — it improves
  geographic clarity + entity modeling + AI legibility. Put `LocalBusiness` + `areaServed` JSON-LD on each
  location page. [confirmed] → https://schema.org/areaServed · https://seonorth.ca/schema/areaserved/
- **Keep the map embed off the critical path** (static-image facade) — it's a CWV/LCP/INP liability at scale.
  See the new prompt `areas-maps/prompts/06-core-web-vitals-hardening.md`.

## 6. What changed in 2025–2026 (don't ship stale advice)

1. **AI Local Packs are live** (mobile, US, ~7% of local keywords as of Dec 2025): they show **only 1–2
   businesses (vs 3), surface ~32% as many unique businesses as the classic 3-pack, and have NO call button.**
   In 88% of 322 markets analyzed, fewer businesses appeared. → Plan for **fewer pack slots + a website/AI
   citation conversion path**, not just pack presence. [directional] → https://www.sterlingsky.ca/the-state-of-local-seo-in-2026/
2. **"AI Search Visibility" became a scored ranking category** where on-page (24%) > GBP (12%) — see §3.
3. **Review velocity now beats raw count** (the 18-day-pause finding) — a real 2025 shift from "accumulate total reviews."
4. **Prominence redefined toward real-world engagement** (brand searches, GBP clicks, direction requests) — a
   newer business with high engagement can outrank a higher-authority competitor. [directional]
5. **Spam enforcement hardened** (Feb 2025 + Aug 26 2025) specifically against templated/AI city pages.
6. **GBP business-title keyword-stuffing → more suspensions in 2026** (title keywords still rank, but the risk rose).
7. **AI Overviews are displacing informational local clicks** — transactional "[service] in [city]" intent is
   where SAB pages still convert; route informational queries to the blog cluster instead. [directional]

## 7. Highest-leverage actions, ranked

1. **One indexable URL per service-area you can genuinely serve and prove — not per theoretical city.** Stay in
   the deliberate "few dozen → low hundreds" zone; penalty risk scales with page-count × template-similarity.
2. **Force ≥1–2 genuinely unique first-party local elements per page** before publish (the 4-of-8 gate). This is
   simultaneously the doorway-vs-helpful differentiator **and** the proven traffic driver.
3. **Never orphan a page** — wire every SAP into global nav + an Areas hub + relevant service/blog pages with
   descriptive geo anchors. Orphaning is an explicit doorway signal.
4. **Win the pack through GBP, not pages:** nail the **primary category**, **show the verified address**, run
   **continuous monthly review velocity with text reviews** (never stall 2+ weeks).
5. **Match intent to page type:** transactional → SAP; informational → blog cluster that links *down* into SAPs.
6. **Add `LocalBusiness` + `areaServed` JSON-LD per page**; embed a map only where legitimate, off the critical path.
7. **Optimize the page for AI Search Visibility now** (on-page is 24% there): structured, factual, locally
   specific copy is what gets the page cited as AI discovery replaces lost pack slots and organic clicks.
