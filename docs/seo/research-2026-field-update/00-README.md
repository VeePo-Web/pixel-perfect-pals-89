## How to read a fact in here

Every load-bearing claim carries a **source URL + date** and, where relevant, a confidence tag:

- **[confirmed]** — primary source or corroborated across independent studies (treat as durable).
- **[directional]** — vendor/practitioner study; the *pattern* is reliable, the *exact %* is method-dependent and may already have moved.
- **[volatile]** — a fast-moving figure already revised at least once in 2025–2026; cite only with its date.

The single most important meta-lesson of this update: **AI-search numbers move fast.** Several 2025 headline
figures were overturned by early 2026 (e.g. AIO↔top-10 overlap 76% → 38%; ChatGPT's Reddit citation share
collapsed ~60% → ~10% in six weeks). Build the template on the **confirmed structural facts** (no-JS crawlers,
answer-first chunks, schema-in-HTML, freshness, the publish gate) and treat the percentages as dials, not constants.
