## Calgary Region

- **CLIMATE:** Semi-arid prairie/foothills transition. Chinook winds bring dramatic winter freeze-thaw swings (temperatures can rise 15-20C in hours); among Canada's sunniest regions; the Calgary-Airdrie corridor sits in Canada's most active hailstorm belt ("hailstorm alley"), with summer hail a recurring property risk. Cold snaps to -30C still occur.
- **GEOLOGY/SOIL:** Dark brown chernozem prairie soils east; foothills rising west toward the Rockies; Bow and Elbow river valleys.
- **ECONOMY:** Canada's energy-sector head-office capital (downtown Calgary); logistics/distribution (CrossIron Mills and warehousing at Balzac); ranching and equestrian acreages in the foothills; rapid bedroom-community growth (Airdrie, Chestermere, Cochrane, Okotoks all among Canada's fastest-growing municipalities in recent censuses).
- **BUILDING STOCK:** Inner-Calgary postwar bungalows; 1980s-2000s vinyl/stucco suburbs; new master-planned communities; country-residential acreages (Springbank, Bearspaw, De Winton, Bragg Creek); main-street brick in the older towns.
- **LANDMARKS:** Bow River, Elbow River, Calgary Tower, Calgary Stampede grounds, Nose Hill Park, CrossIron Mills (Balzac), WinSport/Canada Olympic Park, Big Hill Springs Provincial Park (near Cochrane).

## Edmonton Region

- **CLIMATE:** Humid continental; colder, longer winters than Calgary with far less chinook moderation; deep ground frost, significant snow loads, -30C to -40C cold snaps; strong summer thunderstorms. Freeze-thaw shoulder seasons are hard on exteriors, concrete and roofs.
- **GEOLOGY/SOIL:** Black chernozem soils (some of Canada's most productive farmland); North Saskatchewan River valley cuts through the metro - the largest stretch of urban parkland in Canada.
- **ECONOMY:** Provincial government and University of Alberta (Edmonton); petrochemical refining and upgrading in "Alberta's Industrial Heartland" (Fort Saskatchewan-Sturgeon County-Bruderheim area, incl. the Sherwood Park refinery corridor); the Nisku/Leduc energy-services and logistics corridor by Edmonton International Airport; West Edmonton Mall; CFB Edmonton (north side, near Gibbons/Bon Accord side).
- **BUILDING STOCK:** Mature postwar bungalow neighbourhoods; 1970s-90s suburbs; fast-growing new subdivisions (Beaumont, Spruce Grove, Leduc, Fort Saskatchewan); acreage belts in Sturgeon and Parkland counties; grain-town main streets further out (Lamont, Bruderheim, Vegreville corridor).
- **LANDMARKS:** North Saskatchewan River valley, West Edmonton Mall, Alberta Legislature, Elk Island National Park (east, near Lamont/Tofield), Edmonton International Airport (Leduc), Vegreville's giant Pysanka (world's largest Ukrainian easter egg).

## Central Alberta

- **CLIMATE:** Continental aspen-parkland climate; cold winters with multi-week -30C snaps, warm summers with hail-bearing thunderstorms; pronounced freeze-thaw in April/October. Less chinook influence than the south.
- **GEOLOGY/SOIL:** Black and dark-brown chernozem - among Canada's most productive cropland; aspen parkland transitioning to boreal forest northwest and badlands east (Drumheller's Red Deer River valley exposes dinosaur-bearing badlands).
- **ECONOMY:** Agriculture (canola, wheat, cattle, dairy) plus oil & gas services; the Highway 2 (QE2) corridor between Calgary and Edmonton is Canada's busiest inter-city corridor, with Red Deer at the midpoint (Gasoline Alley retail/service strip); Sylvan Lake is central Alberta's beach-resort town; Drumheller runs on badlands tourism (Royal Tyrrell Museum) ; east-central towns (Camrose, Killam, Viking, Wainwright) anchor the Battle River farm belt; CFB Wainwright is a major army training base.
- **BUILDING STOCK:** Early-1900s brick main streets (Lacombe's Edwardian downtown is among Alberta's best-preserved); postwar bungalows; lake cottages and new resort builds (Sylvan Lake, Bentley area); modern QE2-corridor suburbs (Blackfalds, Penhold among Alberta's fastest-growing towns); farmsteads and acreages throughout.
- **LANDMARKS:** Red Deer River and badlands (Drumheller hoodoos, Royal Tyrrell Museum), Sylvan Lake waterfront, Gaetz Lakes Sanctuary (Red Deer), Lacombe heritage downtown, Olds College (smart-agriculture college), CFB Wainwright.

## Southern Alberta

- **CLIMATE:** Canada's chinook belt - frequent warm winter winds and constant strong wind overall (Lethbridge and Pincher Creek are among Canada's windiest places); semi-arid, sunny, repeated winter freeze-thaw cycling that is brutal on roofs, siding, and concrete.
- **GEOLOGY/SOIL:** Brown prairie soils, coulee-cut river valleys (Oldman River coulees at Lethbridge are iconic); Rocky Mountain front rises at Pincher Creek/Cardston edge.
- **ECONOMY:** Irrigated agriculture is the engine - the St. Mary River Irrigation District is Canada's largest, feeding sugar beets, potatoes, corn and beans (Taber is famous across Canada for its corn; Lantic's Taber plant is Canada's only sugar-beet factory); "Feedlot Alley" around Picture Butte and Coaldale is Canada's densest cattle-feeding area; wind energy - Cowley Ridge near Pincher Creek was Canada's first commercial wind farm (1993) and turbines now line the region; Lethbridge anchors with university (ULethbridge), college, and agri-food processing.
- **BUILDING STOCK:** Historic brick downtowns (Fort Macleod's is a designated Provincial Historic Area); Mormon-settlement-era heritage homes in Cardston, Raymond, and Magrath; postwar bungalows; new suburbs in west Lethbridge and Coaldale/Coalhurst commuter towns; farm and feedlot buildings throughout.
- **LANDMARKS:** Oldman River coulees + Lethbridge High Level Bridge (world's longest and highest trestle bridge), Head-Smashed-In Buffalo Jump (UNESCO, near Fort Macleod), Cardston Alberta Temple (1923, first LDS temple outside the USA), Nikka Yuko Japanese Garden (Lethbridge), Waterton Lakes National Park (southwest, near Pincher Creek/Cardston).

## Southeast Alberta

- **CLIMATE:** Alberta's driest, sunniest corner - the heart of Palliser's Triangle; semi-arid, hot summers, strong sun exposure (Medicine Hat is one of Canada's sunniest cities), chinook-moderated winters with sharp cold snaps.
- **GEOLOGY/SOIL:** Brown prairie soils, shortgrass rangeland, South Saskatchewan River valley and badlands coulees.
- **ECONOMY:** Medicine Hat - "The Gas City" - sits atop historic natural-gas fields (once giving it the cheapest energy in Canada) with a clay-products heritage (the Medalta potteries district); Brooks runs on the Eastern Irrigation District (one of Canada's largest) and the JBS Foods plant - Canada's largest beef-packing facility - which makes it one of Canada's most culturally diverse small cities; Bassano's landmark is the Bassano Dam (1914, CPR-built) feeding the EID canals; Bow Island anchors dryland-to-irrigated bean and pulse growing ("Bean Capital of the West").
- **BUILDING STOCK:** Clay-brick heritage in Medicine Hat, postwar bungalows, newer south-Medicine Hat suburbs and Redcliff commuter growth, workforce housing in Brooks, farm/ranch buildings across the rangeland.
- **LANDMARKS:** Saamis Tepee + Medalta Potteries (Medicine Hat), South Saskatchewan River valley, Bassano Dam, Eastern Irrigation District canals, Red Rock Coulee; Dinosaur Provincial Park (UNESCO) lies northeast of Brooks.

## Canadian Rockies

- TWO DISTINCT ZONES - use the right one per town.
- (1) BOW VALLEY (Banff, Canmore): Alpine mountain-valley climate - heavy snow loads, rapid freeze-thaw, intense UV at 1,300-1,400 m elevation. Banff townsite sits INSIDE Banff National Park - Canada's first national park (1885) - under federal need-to-reside rules and strict development caps/leasehold land; tourism is essentially the entire economy (Banff Ave, Fairmont Banff Springs, Lake Louise up-valley). Canmore, just outside the park gates, boomed after hosting 1988 Winter Olympics nordic events - Three Sisters peaks, Canmore Nordic Centre; building stock is mountain-resort: timber-and-stone hotels, alpine chalets, condo resorts, some of Alberta's highest property values.
- (2) YELLOWHEAD CORRIDOR FOOTHILLS (Hinton, Edson, Whitecourt): Boreal-foothills resource towns along Highway 16/43 - long cold winters, heavy snowfall, muskeg and forest terrain. Hinton: pulp mill town (mill operating since 1957) and gateway to Jasper National Park (~80 km west); Edson: forestry, coal-branch heritage and oil/gas services on Highway 16; Whitecourt: sawmill/pulp forestry hub at the Athabasca-McLeod river confluence, calls itself the "Snowmobile Capital of Alberta". Building stock: wartime/postwar mill-town bungalows, mobile-home stock, newer workforce subdivisions.
- **LANDMARKS:** Banff National Park, Fairmont Banff Springs, Three Sisters (Canmore), Canmore Nordic Centre, Jasper gateway (Hinton), Athabasca River (Whitecourt).

## Northern Alberta

- **CLIMATE:** Boreal continental - long, hard winters (-35C snaps, deep frost, heavy snow), short warm summers; wildfire smoke and wildfire risk are real, recurring factors.
- **GEOLOGY/SOIL:** Boreal forest and muskeg north; grey-wooded and degraded black soils in the farm fringe (Westlock, Barrhead, High Prairie are mixed-farm country - canola, hay, cattle).
- **ECONOMY:** Forestry (sawmills/OSB), oil & gas, agriculture-fringe farming; Slave Lake is a forestry+energy town on Lesser Slave Lake famous province-wide for the 2011 wildfire that destroyed about a third of the town and forced a full modern rebuild (roofing/fire-code relevance); Athabasca is the historic "Gateway to the North" river-landing town (Athabasca University is based there); Peace River town sits dramatically in the Peace River valley; High Prairie anchors the Lesser Slave farm belt.
- **BUILDING STOCK:** Post-2011 rebuilt homes in Slave Lake (modern codes), wartime/postwar bungalows, farmhouses, mobile homes, main-street commercial from the rail era; Athabasca has notable Edwardian riverfront heritage buildings.
- **LANDMARKS:** Lesser Slave Lake (+ boreal bird observatory), Athabasca River landing, Peace River valley viewpoints, Athabasca University.

## Peace Region

- **CLIMATE:** Boreal but with surprisingly productive long summer daylight; long cold winters, short intense growing season - the world's northernmost major grain belt.
- **ECONOMY:** Grande Prairie ("Swan City", for the trumpeter swans of nearby Saskatoon Island) is the region's hub and one of Canada's youngest, fastest-growing cities - Montney natural-gas/condensate drilling, forestry (large pulp/OSB operations), agriculture, and regional retail/health services for a huge trading area spilling into BC.
- **BUILDING STOCK:** Newer workforce suburbs (much of the housing stock is post-1990), big-box retail corridors, acreages, farmsteads.
- **LANDMARKS:** Muskoseepi Park, Saskatoon Island Provincial Park, Grande Prairie Regional College (Douglas Cardinal-designed).

## Wood Buffalo Region

- **CLIMATE:** Subarctic boreal - among the coldest winters of any sizeable Canadian urban area (-40C snaps), short summers, serious wildfire exposure.
- **ECONOMY:** Fort McMurray is the urban service area of the Regional Municipality of Wood Buffalo and the operational capital of the Athabasca oil sands (Suncor, Syncrude and others north of town); fly-in/drive-in workforce; province-leading household incomes. The 2016 Horse River wildfire ("The Beast") forced the largest wildfire evacuation in Canadian history (~88,000 people) and destroyed ~2,400 structures - whole neighbourhoods (Beacon Hill, Abasand, Waterways) have been rebuilt to modern codes since.
- **GEOGRAPHY:** Sits at the confluence of the Athabasca and Clearwater rivers, cut by steep forested valley slopes.
- **BUILDING STOCK:** Post-2016 rebuilt homes in fire-affected neighbourhoods, 1970s-80s boom-era subdivisions (Thickwood, Timberlea), workforce condos/apartments, mobile-home parks.
- **LANDMARKS:** Athabasca + Clearwater rivers, oil-sands plants north on Highway 63, MacDonald Island Park (Canada's largest community recreation centre).

## Lakeland Region

- **CLIMATE:** Boreal-parkland transition - cold snowy winters, warm summers; the lake country brings cottage/seasonal-property dynamics.
- **ECONOMY:** Cold Lake hosts 4 Wing CFB Cold Lake - Canada's busiest fighter-jet base - plus Imperial Oil's Cold Lake in-situ oil-sands operation (one of the largest thermal heavy-oil projects in the world) and lake tourism on Alberta's seventh-largest lake; Bonnyville services heavy-oil and farm country between Cold Lake and St. Paul; Lloydminster is Canada's only city straddling two provinces (the Alberta-Saskatchewan border runs down 50 Avenue/Meridian Avenue) with a heavy-oil economy (Husky/Cenovus upgrader on the Saskatchewan side); Vermilion is a college town (Lakeland College, founded 1913) and agricultural service centre; Lac La Biche is a historic Metis and fur-trade community (Lac La Biche Mission, 1853) on its namesake lake.
- **BUILDING STOCK:** Military housing + newer subdivisions (Cold Lake), lakefront cottages and cabins, postwar bungalows, farm buildings; Lloydminster's bi-provincial downtown.
- **LANDMARKS:** Cold Lake (the lake) + Kinosoo Ridge, 4 Wing air base, Lloydminster border markers on Meridian Avenue, Lakeland College (Vermilion), Lac La Biche Mission National Historic Site, Sir Winston Churchill Provincial Park.
