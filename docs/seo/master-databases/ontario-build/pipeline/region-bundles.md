# ONTARIO REGION FACT BUNDLES (computed once — shared grounding; attribute these facts to the REGION, never to a single place; never copy a sentence verbatim into two pages)

## Greater Toronto Area
Climate: humid continental (Dfa/Dfb); hot humid summers, freeze-thaw winters with lake-effect variation; ~80–110 cm snow; spring thaw and summer downpours stress older drainage. Geography: Lake Ontario shoreline rising to the Oak Ridges Moraine; ravine systems (Don, Humber, Rouge, Credit). Economy: Canada's largest metro economy — finance, tech, logistics/Pearson corridor, construction; among the fastest-growing municipalities in Canada. Building stock: everything from pre-war brick and post-war bungalows to glass condo towers and new greenfield subdivisions; intense infill/teardown activity. Anchors: Lake Ontario waterfront, Hwy 401/407/QEW network, GO Transit rail web, Oak Ridges Moraine/Greenbelt, Rouge National Urban Park.

## Southwestern Ontario
Climate: among Ontario's mildest and most humid; hot summers, frequent freeze-thaw cycles, snow squalls off Lakes Huron and Erie in a defined snowbelt. Geography: flat, deep-soil farmland between three Great Lakes (Huron, Erie, St. Clair); Thames and Grand river basins. Economy: agriculture (corn, soy, greenhouse), agri-food processing, auto/advanced manufacturing, universities (Western, insurance HQs in London). Building stock: century farmhouses, yellow-brick Victorian small-town cores, post-war suburbs, new subdivisions around London/Woodstock. Anchors: Lake Erie and Lake Huron shorelines, Hwy 401/402 corridors, Grand and Thames rivers, Stratford Festival, Port Stanley/Grand Bend beach towns.

## Grey-Bruce-Simcoe
Climate: heavy lake-effect snowbelt off Georgian Bay/Lake Huron (150–300+ cm snow in spots); short humid summers; hard freeze-thaw shoulder seasons. Geography: Niagara Escarpment ridge, Georgian Bay coastline, Bruce Peninsula karst, Simcoe County lake plains around Lake Simcoe/Couchiching. Economy: four-season tourism (ski at Blue Mountain, beaches at Wasaga/Sauble, Bruce Trail), agriculture, Bruce Power (largest operating nuclear plant in the world) on Lake Huron, fast commuter growth in south Simcoe. Building stock: cottages/chalets and waterfront homes, small-town brick cores, booming new subdivisions (Barrie–Innisfil–Bradford). Anchors: Georgian Bay, Niagara Escarpment/Bruce Trail, Blue Mountains, Lake Simcoe, Hwy 400/26.

## Eastern Ontario
Climate: colder continental; real winters (ice storms, 180+ cm snow east of Kingston), humid summers; freeze-thaw hard on masonry. Geography: limestone plains along Lake Ontario/St. Lawrence, Thousand Islands, Rideau Canal corridor lakes, Canadian Shield edge north of Hwy 7. Economy: public sector and military (Kingston: Queen's, RMC, CFB), healthcare, tourism (Thousand Islands, Rideau — a UNESCO World Heritage canal), agriculture. Building stock: signature limestone heritage buildings, red-brick Victorian towns, waterfront cottages, growing suburbs around Kingston/Belleville. Anchors: St. Lawrence River, Rideau Canal, Lake Ontario's Bay of Quinte, Hwy 401, Prince Edward County wine country.

## Ottawa-Eastern
Climate: one of the coldest G7-capital climates — long snowy winters (~220 cm), ice-storm risk, humid summers; deep frost lines. Geography: Ottawa River valley, Rideau River/Canal, Gatineau Hills across the river; flat clay plains east toward the Quebec border (note Leda clay soils — settlement/foundation issues are a known regional condition). Economy: federal government, tech (Kanata North — Canada's largest tech park), defence, healthcare, agriculture in Prescott-Russell/Stormont; strongly bilingual communities east of Ottawa. Building stock: heritage stone/brick in old Ottawa and valley towns, vast post-war and new suburbs (Orléans, Barrhaven, Kanata), rural farmhouses. Anchors: Parliament Hill, Ottawa River, Rideau Canal (UNESCO), Hwy 417, Ottawa Valley.

## Central Ontario
Climate: continental with heavy snow bands off Georgian Bay to the west and 130–200 cm typical; cottage-country freeze-thaw; summer humidity. Geography: Kawartha Lakes and Trent–Severn Waterway (386 km national historic canal), Muskoka's Canadian Shield granite and 1,600+ lakes, Rice Lake plains, Northumberland Hills. Economy: cottage/tourism economy (Muskoka is one of North America's best-known lake districts), retirees and remote workers, agriculture south of Rice Lake, Trent University/Fleming College in Peterborough. Building stock: lakefront cottages (increasingly converted to year-round), century homes in canal towns, new builds around Peterborough/Lindsay. Anchors: Trent–Severn Waterway, Kawartha Lakes, Muskoka lakes, Algonquin Park's southern gateway, Hwy 115/35/11.

## Kitchener-Waterloo
Climate: humid continental; snowbelt-edge winters (~160 cm), freeze-thaw, humid summers. Geography: rolling Grand River watershed farmland; Waterloo Moraine aquifers. Economy: Canada's densest tech cluster outside Toronto (Communitech, Google Kitchener, U Waterloo, Laurier, Perimeter Institute), insurance HQs (Manulife, Sun Life, Economical), advanced manufacturing (Toyota Cambridge), Mennonite farm economy in Woolwich/Wellesley townships. Building stock: yellow-brick Victorians, post-war neighbourhoods, condo boom along the ION LRT line, farmhouses and shops in St. Jacobs/Elmira country. Anchors: Grand River (Canadian Heritage River), ION LRT, Hwy 401/7/8, University of Waterloo/Guelph, St. Jacobs Farmers' Market, Kitchener-Waterloo Oktoberfest.

## Hamilton-Niagara
Climate: one of Ontario's mildest (peach/grape-growing tender-fruit belt), but sharp freeze-thaw and lake-effect off Lake Ontario/Erie; escarpment microclimates. Geography: Niagara Escarpment (UNESCO biosphere) cutting through Hamilton ("the Mountain") to Niagara Falls; more than 100 waterfalls in Hamilton alone; Welland Canal linking Lakes Ontario and Erie. Economy: steel and advanced manufacturing (Stelco, ArcelorMittal Dofasco), McMaster University and Hamilton Health Sciences, Niagara's wine and tourism industry (14M+ visitors/yr to Niagara Falls), St. Catharines' Brock University. Building stock: pre-war brick worker housing, escarpment-view suburbs, vineyard estates, canal-town cores, high-rise renewal in downtown Hamilton/St. Catharines. Anchors: Niagara Falls, Welland Canal, Niagara Escarpment/Bruce Trail southern terminus, QEW corridor, Niagara wine region.

## Windsor-Essex
Climate: Canada's southernmost — mildest winters in Ontario, hottest summers, high humidity, tornado-alley edge; short freeze season but violent storms and heavy rains. Geography: flat lake-plain peninsula between Lake St. Clair, the Detroit River, and Lake Erie; Point Pelee National Park is mainland Canada's southernmost point. Economy: auto capital of Canada (Stellantis Windsor, new EV battery plants), the continent's busiest commercial border crossing (Ambassador Bridge/Gordie Howe Bridge), North America's greenhouse capital in Leamington/Kingsville, Lake Erie wineries. Building stock: modest post-war bungalows, new suburbs (LaSalle, Lakeshore, Tecumseh), farm properties and greenhouse operations. Anchors: Detroit River skyline, Lake Erie shoreline, Point Pelee, Hwy 401 terminus, EPICentre/University of Windsor.

## Northern Ontario
Climate: the province's harshest — long severe winters (Sudbury/Thunder Bay ~200–280 cm snow, −30 °C snaps), short intense summers, permafrost pockets far north; freeze-thaw and frost heave dominate building maintenance. Geography: Canadian Shield granite, boreal forest, thousands of lakes (Lake Nipissing, Lake Superior's north shore, Lake of the Woods); vast distances between centres. Economy: mining (Sudbury's nickel basin is one of the world's largest, Vale/Glencore), forestry, steel (Algoma in Sault Ste. Marie), grain/port logistics (Thunder Bay), healthcare/education hubs (Laurentian, Lakehead, NOSM), Indigenous communities and traditional territories across the region. Building stock: wartime housing in resource towns, lakefront camps/cottages, downtown brick cores, industrial facilities. Anchors: Lake Superior, Trans-Canada Hwy 11/17, Sudbury's Big Nickel/Science North, Sleeping Giant, Agawa Canyon.
