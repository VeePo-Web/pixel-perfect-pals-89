## About Kedgwick, New Brunswick

Kedgwick is a forestry rural community of 1,986 people (2021 census) in the Restigouche uplands of northern New Brunswick. It serves as an access point to the Restigouche River's famous canoe and salmon waters. Spread across a vast 649 square kilometres of high timberland, it is a working forest community with deep-woods winters. Set 15 km from Saint-Quentin, it is one of the most sparsely settled municipalities in the province, at just over three people per square kilometre.
