## About New Brunswick

New Brunswick is Canada's only officially bilingual province, with a 2021 census population of 775,610.
It runs from the Bay of Fundy — home to the highest tides on Earth — north to Chaleur Bay and the
Acadian Peninsula, with the Saint John River (Wolastoq) threading the length of its western side. Its
three largest urban centres are Moncton, Saint John and Fredericton; its economy spans the port and
refinery in Saint John, government and universities in Fredericton, logistics in Greater Moncton, and
forestry, fisheries and potato farming everywhere between.

## Our seven New Brunswick service regions

- **[Greater Moncton & Southeast](/areas/region/greater-moncton-southeast)** — the fastest-growing urban
  region in Atlantic Canada: Moncton, Dieppe, Riverview, the Shediac shore and Kent County.
- **[Fundy & Greater Saint John](/areas/region/fundy-saint-john)** — Canada's first city, the
  Kennebecasis Valley towns, and Charlotte County's coast and islands.
- **[Capital & River Valley](/areas/region/capital-river-valley)** — Fredericton, Oromocto and the
  Saint John River valley up through the Carleton County potato belt.
- **[Northwest & Madawaska](/areas/region/northwest-madawaska)** — Edmundston, Grand Falls and the
  Brayon border country of the upper Saint John.
- **[Miramichi River Region](/areas/region/miramichi-river)** — the famous salmon river, its city and
  its headwater communities.
- **[Acadian Peninsula & Chaleur](/areas/region/acadian-peninsula-chaleur)** — Bathurst, Caraquet,
  Shippagan and the francophone shore of Chaleur Bay.
- **[Restigouche & Chaleur Bay](/areas/region/restigouche-chaleur)** — Campbellton, Dalhousie and the
  Appalachian high country.
