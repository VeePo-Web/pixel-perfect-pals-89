## About Miramichi, New Brunswick

Miramichi is a city of 17,692 people (2021 census) in Northumberland County, New Brunswick, created in 1995 by amalgamating Chatham, Newcastle, Douglastown, Loggieville and Nelson. Twin historic town sites face each other across the broad Miramichi River, about 110 km north of Moncton. World-renowned for Atlantic salmon angling, the city holds the Beaubears Island national historic sites, Ritchie Wharf park and French Fort Cove, and has been rebuilding its economy since the pulp-mill closures of the 2000s.
