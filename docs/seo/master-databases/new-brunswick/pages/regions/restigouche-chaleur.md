## About the Restigouche & Chaleur Bay

The Restigouche & Chaleur Bay region is the most rugged corner of New Brunswick, occupying Restigouche County where the Appalachian mountains drop to the Restigouche River estuary and Chaleur Bay. Campbellton, the border city under Sugarloaf, is the gateway to Quebec's Gaspé Peninsula by the J.C. Van Horne Bridge; Dalhousie has been reinventing itself since its paper mill closed in 2008; and Saint-Quentin claims the title of maple capital of Atlantic Canada. This is forestry country and world-famous Restigouche salmon water, with landmarks from Sugarloaf Provincial Park to the Inch Arran lighthouse.
