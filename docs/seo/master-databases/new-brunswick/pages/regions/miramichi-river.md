## About the Miramichi River Region

The Miramichi River Region follows the great Miramichi River system through Northumberland County, its Main Southwest and Northwest branches draining a vast forested interior to Miramichi Bay on the Gulf of St. Lawrence. The river is one of the world's storied Atlantic salmon fisheries, and the region's economy is built on forestry and the angling trade. The city of Miramichi, formed in 1995 from the rival towns of Chatham and Newcastle, anchors it, rebuilding since the 2000s mill closures. Landmarks range from the Beaubears Island national historic sites to the Central New Brunswick Woodsmen's Museum at Boiestown.
