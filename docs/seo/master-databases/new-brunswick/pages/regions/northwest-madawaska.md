## About the Northwest & Madawaska

The Northwest & Madawaska is the coldest, snowiest corner of New Brunswick, spread across Madawaska and Victoria counties along the upper Saint John River. Edmundston anchors it, an overwhelmingly francophone city with a distinct Brayon identity built around the Twin Rivers pulp mill and a Université de Moncton campus. The region's landmarks include the Grand Falls gorge and its 23-metre cataract, the Petit-Témis cycling trail, and the New Brunswick Botanical Garden. Its economy runs on pulp and paper, cross-border trade with Maine and Quebec, and the upper-valley potato industry.

## Communities we serve in the Northwest & Madawaska

- [Edmundston](/areas/edmundston) — the francophone Brayon capital (16,437 people), wrapped around the Twin Rivers pulp mill at the Madawaska–Saint John junction.
- [Grand Falls / Grand-Sault](/areas/grand-falls) — a bilingual town of 5,220 built beside the Saint John River's 23-metre cataract and mile-long gorge.
- [Haut-Madawaska](/areas/haut-madawaska) — 3,720 people across border villages including Clair and Baker-Brook, facing Maine across the upper Saint John.
- [Saint-André](/areas/saint-andre) — a potato-farming community of 1,794 on the upper-valley benchlands, 9 km from Grand Falls.
