## About the Acadian Peninsula & Chaleur

The Acadian Peninsula & Chaleur region is the francophone heart of Acadie, occupying Gloucester County along Chaleur Bay and the Gulf of St. Lawrence. This is low peninsula country of beaches, salt marsh, barrier islands and the largest peat bogs in the province. Its economy runs on snow-crab and lobster fleets, peat harvesting around Shippagan and Lamèque, fish processing, and a strong cultural-tourism economy centred on Caraquet; Bathurst, the regional service city, has been diversifying since the Brunswick zinc mine closed in 2013. Landmarks include the Village Historique Acadien, the Festival acadien de Caraquet, and the New Brunswick Aquarium and Marine Centre at Shippagan.

## Communities we serve in the Acadian Peninsula & Chaleur

- [Tracadie](/areas/tracadie) — New Brunswick's first regional municipality (16,043 people), a fishing and peat-country service centre on the Gulf shore.
- [Bathurst](/areas/bathurst) — the Chaleur Bay service city of 12,157 at the mouth of the Nepisiguit, home to the K.C. Irving Regional Centre.
- [Caraquet](/areas/caraquet) — the cultural capital of Acadie, a fishing town of 4,285 hosting the Festival acadien and August 15 Tintamarre.
- [Beresford](/areas/beresford) — a beach town of 4,294 on the Chaleur shore just north of Bathurst, known for Beresford Beach.
- [Shippagan](/areas/shippagan) — the peat capital and snow-crab port of 2,672 at the Peninsula's tip, home to the NB Aquarium.
- [Lamèque](/areas/lameque) — an island town of 1,301 ringed by peat bogs, host of the International Baroque Music Festival.
- [Petit-Rocher](/areas/petit-rocher) — an Acadian wharf village of 1,954 on the Chaleur shore between Bathurst and Belledune.
