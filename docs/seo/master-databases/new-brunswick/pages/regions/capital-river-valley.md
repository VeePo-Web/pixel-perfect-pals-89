## About the Capital & River Valley

The Capital & River Valley follows the broad Saint John River (Wolastoq) through York, Sunbury, Queens, and Carleton counties. Fredericton anchors it with provincial government, two universities (UNB and St. Thomas), an IT and cybersecurity sector, and RCMP J Division headquarters; Oromocto hosts CFB Gagetown, Canada's largest military training base; and Carleton County upriver is the potato belt, home of McCain Foods in Florenceville-Bristol. Landmarks range from Fredericton's Garrison District and the Beaverbrook Art Gallery to the 391 m Hartland Covered Bridge, the world's longest.

## One valley, many kinds of property

This region's climate writes the work order. The valley has a continental river-valley climate — cold snowy winters, warm summers — and a spring freshet on the Saint John River that produced major floods in 2018 and 2019, so flood-zone awareness is a real factor for riverfront property work from Gagetown to the potato belt. {COMPANY_NAME} builds that into every riverfront quote: elevation and exposure assessed first, {ESTIMATE_TYPE} estimates second, work sequenced around the freshet window.

The building stock is just as varied as the geography — fertile floodplain and island farmland, forested Appalachian foothills, and the Mactaquac headpond above Fredericton. Government-town heritage streets and mid-century neighbourhoods fill Fredericton; Oromocto carries planned 1950s–60s housing built for the base; Hanwell and New Maryland are new commuter subdivisions; and upriver it is farm properties and woodlot homes. ## Communities we serve in the Capital & River Valley

- [Fredericton](/areas/fredericton) — the provincial capital (63,116 people), pairing government with UNB, St. Thomas, and an IT and cybersecurity sector.
- [Oromocto](/areas/oromocto) — 9,045 people and home of CFB Gagetown, Canada's largest military training base, with planned 1950s–60s neighbourhoods.
- [Woodstock](/areas/woodstock) — a town of 5,553 in the upper valley, 18 km from Hartland's covered bridge.
- [Hanwell](/areas/hanwell) — 4,743 people in fast-growing commuter subdivisions on the capital's edge.
- [New Maryland](/areas/new-maryland) — a commuter community of 4,153 just south of Fredericton.
- [Minto](/areas/minto) — 2,234 people, about 35 km from the shiretown of Gagetown.
- [Chipman](/areas/chipman) — a community of 1,201 residents at the 2021 census.
- [Florenceville-Bristol](/areas/florenceville-bristol) — 1,573 people and the headquarters of McCain Foods in the potato belt.
- [McAdam](/areas/mcadam) — a railway village of 1,173 whose chateau-style CPR station is a National Historic Site.
- [Nackawic](/areas/nackawic) — a 1960s mill town of 962 on the Mactaquac headpond, home of the World's Largest Axe.
- [Gagetown](/areas/gagetown) — the historic Queens County shiretown, 787 people, artisan studios, and a cable ferry to Jemseg.
- [Cambridge-Narrows](/areas/cambridge-narrows) — 715 people spanning the narrows of Washademoak Lake, where 45% of dwellings are seasonal.
- [Fredericton Junction](/areas/fredericton-junction) — a rail-heritage village of 719 on the South Branch Oromocto River.
- [Hartland](/areas/hartland) — 933 people beside the world's longest covered bridge, 391 m across the Saint John.
- [Tracy](/areas/tracy) — a village of 610 in Fredericton's commuter orbit, 6 km from Fredericton Junction.
