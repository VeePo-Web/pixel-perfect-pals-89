## About Edmundston, New Brunswick

Edmundston is a city of 16,437 people (2021 census) in Madawaska County, northwestern New Brunswick, at the junction of the Madawaska and Saint John rivers on the Quebec–Maine border. Overwhelmingly francophone with a distinct Brayon identity, it is known as the capital of the "République du Madawaska." The Twin Rivers Paper pulp mill anchors the economy, the Université de Moncton has a campus here, and the city hosts the Foire Brayonne festival each summer. Edmundston amalgamated Saint-Basile and Saint-Jacques in 1998 and faces Madawaska, Maine, across the Saint John River.
