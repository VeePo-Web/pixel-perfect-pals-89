## About Moncton, New Brunswick

Moncton is a city of 79,470 people (2021 census) in Westmorland County, southeastern New Brunswick, and the fastest-growing urban centre in Atlantic Canada — up 10.5 percent since 2016. Canada's first officially bilingual city, it sits on the tidal Petitcodiac River, whose famous bore rolls right through downtown. Université de Moncton, the largest French-language university outside Quebec, anchors a bilingual workforce alongside Medavie Blue Cross, two regional hospitals and the CN rail yard. Magnetic Hill and the Avenir Centre are its best-known landmarks.
