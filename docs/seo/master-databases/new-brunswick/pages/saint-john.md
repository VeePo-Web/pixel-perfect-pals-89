## About Saint John, New Brunswick

Saint John is a port city of 69,895 (2021 census) on the Bay of Fundy at the mouth of the Saint John River. Incorporated in 1785, it was Canada's first city. Today it anchors New Brunswick's industrial economy with the Irving Oil refinery — Canada's largest — and Port Saint John's container and cruise terminals. Landmarks include the Reversing Falls Rapids, the Saint John City Market (Canada's oldest continuing farmers' market), and Rockwood Park, one of Canada's largest urban parks. The city grew 3.4% between 2016 and 2021.
