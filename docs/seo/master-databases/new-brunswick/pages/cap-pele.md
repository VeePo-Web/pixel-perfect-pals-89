## About Cap-Pelé, New Brunswick

Cap-Pelé is a strongly Acadian fishing and cottage village of 2,441 people on the Northumberland Strait in Westmorland County, New Brunswick, 42 km east of Moncton. It is famous for its family herring smokehouses — the "boucanières" — which produce much of the world's smoked herring, and for Aboiteau Beach on some of the warmest salt water north of Virginia. The village spans 22.96 km² of shoreline and back-country.
