## About Dorchester, New Brunswick

Dorchester is the historic shiretown of Westmorland County, New Brunswick — a village of 906 people on a sandstone ridge over the Memramcook estuary marshes, 29 km from Moncton. It is home to Dorchester Penitentiary, one of Canada's oldest federal prisons, the Keillor House Museum, and notable sandstone heritage architecture, with the Johnson's Mills shorebird reserve on the Fundy mudflats nearby.
