## About Memramcook, New Brunswick

Memramcook is a village of 5,029 people (2021 census) in the Memramcook Valley, Westmorland County, New Brunswick — widely regarded as the cradle of the Acadian renaissance. The Monument-Lefebvre National Historic Site and the former Collège Saint-Joseph, the first French-language degree-granting college in Atlantic Canada, both stand here. It is one of the oldest continuously settled Acadian districts, set on fertile farmland along the tidal Memramcook River, 19 km from Moncton. The population grew 5.3 percent from 2016 to 2021.
