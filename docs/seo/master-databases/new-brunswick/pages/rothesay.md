## About Rothesay, New Brunswick

Rothesay is a town of 11,977 (2021 census) in Kings County on the west-facing shore of the Kennebecasis River, 15 km from Saint John. Formed in 1998 by amalgamating Rothesay, Renforth, East Riverside-Kingshurst and Fairvale, it is among New Brunswick's highest-income communities. The town is known for mature riverfront estate lots, Rothesay Common and the wharf area at Renforth, and Rothesay Netherwood School, a historic independent boarding school. Population grew 2.7% between 2016 and 2021.
