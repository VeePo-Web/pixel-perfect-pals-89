## About Saint Andrews, New Brunswick

Saint Andrews is a town of 2,048 people (2021 census) on a Passamaquoddy Bay peninsula in Charlotte County, New Brunswick. One of Canada's oldest seaside resort towns and a National Historic District, it keeps its Loyalist-era street grid, and the Algonquin Resort has hosted guests since 1889. Kingsbrae Garden, whale-watching boats, and the Huntsman Marine Science Centre anchor the visitor economy. The town grew 14.7% between 2016 and 2021 and sits 81 km from Saint John.
