## About Cocagne, New Brunswick

Cocagne is a rural community of 2,757 people (2021 census) in Kent County, New Brunswick, set on the Cocagne River estuary where it opens into the Northumberland Strait. An Acadian harbour community, it is known for its marina and boating harbour and sits along the coastal corridor between Shediac and Bouctouche. The population grew 4.1 percent from 2016 to 2021, and roughly 15 percent of its dwellings are seasonal — a sign of its strong cottage and waterfront character. Moncton is 30 km away.
