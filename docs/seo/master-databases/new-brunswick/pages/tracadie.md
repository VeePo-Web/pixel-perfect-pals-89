## About Tracadie, New Brunswick

Tracadie is a community of 16,043 people (2021 census) in Gloucester County that became New Brunswick's first regional municipality in 2014. It spreads over 516.55 square kilometres of low Gulf-shore coast at the Tracadie River mouth, fringed with beaches and peat bogs, and includes the community of Sheila. A fishing and peat-country service centre for the eastern Acadian Peninsula, Tracadie also holds a singular medical history: Canada's only leprosarium, remembered at the Musée historique de Tracadie.
