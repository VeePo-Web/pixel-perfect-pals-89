## About the South End of Saint John, New Brunswick

The South End is a historic residential quarter of Saint John — Canada's first incorporated city (2021 census population 69,895) — occupying the southern tip of the uptown peninsula in Saint John County, New Brunswick. It is a district of dense Victorian and Edwardian rowhousing, much of it rebuilt after the Great Fire of 1877, fronting the working harbour and the port. Heritage streets and the waterfront define it, minutes below the uptown core.
