## About Marysville, New Brunswick

Marysville is a National Historic Site of Canada and a north-side neighbourhood of Fredericton (the capital's 2021 census population is 63,116), on the Nashwaak River in York County. It was built in the 1800s as a planned cotton-mill company town by Alexander "Boss" Gibson, and its rows of brick mill-worker housing and the great brick cotton mill survive as one of Canada's best-preserved industrial heritage districts. Though amalgamated into Fredericton, it keeps a distinct village identity.
