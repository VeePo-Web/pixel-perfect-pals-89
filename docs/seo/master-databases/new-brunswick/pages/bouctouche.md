## About Bouctouche, New Brunswick

Bouctouche is an Acadian harbour town of 2,513 people (2021 census) on Bouctouche Bay, Kent County, New Brunswick. It is the birthplace of industrialist K.C. Irving, and its cultural landmarks — Le Pays de la Sagouine, the theatrical village celebrating author Antonine Maillet's work, and the Irving Eco-Centre protecting the 12-kilometre Bouctouche dune — draw visitors far out of proportion to its size. The town grew 6.4 percent from 2016 to 2021 and sits 42 km from Moncton on the Northumberland Strait coast.
