## About Nackawic, New Brunswick

Nackawic is a town of 962 people (2021 census) in York County, New Brunswick, set on the Mactaquac headpond of the Saint John River. The town was created in the 1960s when the headpond rose, and the AV Group NBI pulp mill remains its anchor employer. Its waterfront hosts the World's Largest Axe monument. Nackawic covers 7.68 km² with 451 private dwellings, 43 km from Fredericton.
