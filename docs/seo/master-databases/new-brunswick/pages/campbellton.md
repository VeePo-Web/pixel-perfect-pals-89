## About Campbellton, New Brunswick

Campbellton is a city of 7,047 people (2021 census) in Restigouche County, northern New Brunswick, on the Restigouche River estuary at the foot of Sugarloaf Mountain. The J.C. Van Horne Bridge crosses from downtown to Pointe-à-la-Croix, Quebec, making the city the gateway to the Gaspé Peninsula. Sugarloaf Provincial Park and its ski hill rise directly over town, Restigouche Sam — the giant Atlantic salmon statue — celebrates the river's world-famous salmon water, and the population grew 2.4% between 2016 and 2021.
