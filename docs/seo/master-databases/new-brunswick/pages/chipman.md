## About Chipman, New Brunswick

Chipman is a village of 1,201 people (2021 census) in Queens County, New Brunswick, on the Salmon River near the northern end of Grand Lake, the province's largest lake. It grew 8.8% between 2016 and 2021 — strong growth for a rural village — and carries a long history in lumbering and wood processing. Chipman covers 19 km² at 63.2 people per km², with 647 dwellings, about 6.5% of them seasonal or vacant. Moncton is 85 km away and Saint John 102 km.
