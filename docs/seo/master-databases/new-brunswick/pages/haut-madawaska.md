## About Haut-Madawaska, New Brunswick

Haut-Madawaska is a rural community of 3,720 people (2021 census) in Madawaska County, northwestern New Brunswick, formed in 2017 by amalgamating border villages including Clair, Baker-Brook, Saint-Hilaire and Saint-François. It stretches along the upper Saint John River, facing Maine across the water, and covers 611.55 square kilometres of farm and forest land at just 6.1 people per square kilometre. Its population held steady between 2016 and 2021, edging up 0.2%.
