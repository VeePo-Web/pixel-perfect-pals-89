## About Florenceville-Bristol, New Brunswick

Florenceville-Bristol is a town of 1,573 people (2021 census) in Carleton County, New Brunswick, formed in 2008 by merging the villages of Florenceville and Bristol. It is the world headquarters of McCain Foods, the world's largest french-fry producer, and the heart of the Carleton County potato belt. The town sits on benchlands over the Saint John River, covering 15.74 km² at 100 people per km², with 729 dwellings. Woodstock is 33 km downriver; Saint John is 177 km away.
