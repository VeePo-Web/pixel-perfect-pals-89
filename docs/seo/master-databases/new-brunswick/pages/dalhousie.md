## About Dalhousie, New Brunswick

Dalhousie is a port town of 3,223 people (2021 census) on the Restigouche estuary where it opens into Chaleur Bay, Restigouche County, New Brunswick. A deep-water port, it has been reinventing its economy since its paper mill closed in 2008. Its shorefront landmarks include the Inch Arran lighthouse, and a seasonal ferry once linked it to Miguasha, Quebec, across the bay. The town grew 3.1 percent from 2016 to 2021 and sits 22 km from Campbellton.
