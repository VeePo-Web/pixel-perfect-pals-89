## About St. Norbert, Manitoba

St. Norbert is Winnipeg's southernmost district, a historic francophone and Métis community of about 5,850 people where the La Salle River joins the Red. One of the oldest settlements in the region, it was central to the 1869–70 Red River Resistance and preserves the ruins of a Trappist monastery in St. Norbert Provincial Park. Its riverside setting at a river confluence brings real flood exposure, and its blend of heritage and newer homes on Red River clay defines its character.
