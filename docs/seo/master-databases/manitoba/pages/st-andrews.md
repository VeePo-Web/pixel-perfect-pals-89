## About St. Andrews, Manitoba

The Rural Municipality of St. Andrews is a riverfront municipality of 11,723 people (2021 census) on the west bank of the Red River north of Winnipeg, one of the largest RMs in the Interlake. It is defined by the historic River Road corridor, home to Lower Fort Garry — the oldest intact stone fur-trade fort in North America — and St. Andrews-on-the-Red, the oldest stone church in Western Canada still in use. Its riverfront estates, its heritage corridor, and its Red River clay ground define its market.
