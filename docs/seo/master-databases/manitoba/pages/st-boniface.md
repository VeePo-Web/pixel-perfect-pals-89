## About St. Boniface, Manitoba

St. Boniface is Winnipeg's historic French quarter on the east bank of the Red River and the heart of Western Canada's largest francophone community, with about 58,520 residents. Founded as a Catholic mission in 1818 and a separate city until Winnipeg's 1972 amalgamation, it holds the St. Boniface Cathedral façade, Louis Riel's grave, St. Boniface Hospital and the Université de Saint-Boniface. The Seine River winds through its residential streets, and the Esplanade Riel footbridge links Provencher Boulevard to The Forks.
