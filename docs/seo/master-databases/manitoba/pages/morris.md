## About Morris, Manitoba

Morris is a town of 1,975 people (2021 census) on the Red River at its junction with the Morris River, 62 km south of Winnipeg on Highway 75. Famous for hosting the Manitoba Stampede — one of Canada's largest rodeos — it sits behind a ring dike on the Red River floodplain and was an island in the 1997 Flood of the Century. Its ring-diked setting, its deep valley clay, and its role as a Highway 75 corridor town define its residential character.
