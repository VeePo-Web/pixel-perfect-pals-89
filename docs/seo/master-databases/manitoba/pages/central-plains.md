## About Central Plains

The Central Plains is the farm-and-processing belt west and southwest of Winnipeg, anchored by Portage la Prairie (13,270) — Canada's french-fry-and-pea-protein processing capital, with McCain, Simplot and Roquette plants — and stretching across the table-flat, stone-free Portage plains between the Assiniboine River and Lake Manitoba. Its eastern RMs (Macdonald with Oak Bluff and La Salle, Cartier with Elie) form Winnipeg's southwest growth fringe, while St. François Xavier holds the region's Métis river-lot heritage on the Assiniboine.

## Across the Central Plains region

The Central Plains stretches from Winnipeg's southwest doorstep to the shores of Lake Manitoba, and its anchor city earns its keep: Portage la Prairie (13,270) processes a startling share of Canada's potatoes at the McCain and Simplot plants and hosts Roquette's pea-protein facility — among the largest anywhere — plus Southport Aerospace on the former air-base grounds. The city itself wraps around Crescent Lake, an oxbow that puts an island park in the middle of town and gives the historic Crescent-area streets their character. For {SERVICE}, Portage offers the full spread in one municipality of Manitoba: early-1900s homes under mature elms, postwar wards, industrial-park commercial work and RM farmsteads across some of the country's richest, stone-free loam.

The region's eastern edge lives a different life. Macdonald RM's Oak Bluff and La Salle — with Sanford and Starbuck along the La Salle River — have become Winnipeg's southwest estate fringe, new builds on large lots that still sit on the same expansive Red River gumbo as the city next door. Cartier's Elie and the francophone river-lot community of St. François Xavier line the Trans-Canada and the Assiniboine, where the White Horse legend statue marks the highway. Clay-movement planning, rural wells and septic, and long-driveway logistics shape the work here more than anything urban.

Between the two, the Portage plains proper run table-flat between the Assiniboine River and Lake Manitoba — protected from the river's big years by the Portage Diversion channel, and swept by the open-prairie winds that make exterior work planning a real skill. Grey, North Norfolk (MacGregor) and Victoria fill out the farm belt with elevator towns and section-road properties.
