## About Thompson, Manitoba

Thompson is a city of 13,035 people (2021 census) in northern Manitoba, 652 km north of Winnipeg on the Burntwood River. Known as the Hub of the North, it was purpose-built in 1956 when Inco — now Vale — opened the surrounding nickel belt, and mining remains its economic anchor alongside regional healthcare, government, and a University College of the North campus. Set in Canadian Shield boreal forest, its housing stock is largely planned-city construction from the 1950s through 1970s, now in its renewal decades.
