## About Interlake

The Interlake is the lowland between Lake Winnipeg and Lake Manitoba, stretching north from Winnipeg's bedroom RMs (East and West St. Paul, St. Andrews, St. Clements) through Selkirk, Stonewall and Teulon to Gimli, Arborg and the lake shores. Its plain of shallow Ordovician limestone yields Tyndall stone — the fossil-mottled cladding of the Manitoba Legislature — quarried at Garson and Tyndall. Selkirk's Gerdau steel mill, Gimli's Diageo distillery and Lake Winnipeg's commercial fishery headline the economy, alongside Icelandic 'New Iceland' heritage on the lakeshore.

## Across the Interlake region

The Interlake runs north from Winnipeg between its two inland seas — Lake Winnipeg on the east, Lake Manitoba on the west — and for {SERVICE} purposes it is really three territories in one region of Manitoba. The southern tier is metro spillover: East St. Paul and West St. Paul's estate subdivisions, St. Andrews' River Road heritage corridor past Lower Fort Garry (North America's oldest intact stone fur-trade fort), and St. Clements reaching Lockport, where the Red River Floodway returns to the river at the historic lock and dam. Riverbank stability and septic-to-municipal transitions dominate the work here.

The middle tier is quarry and rail country. Selkirk (10,504) is the regional city, its Gerdau mill melting scrap into steel beside a working Red River waterfront; Stonewall and Teulon grew up on the limestone that gives the whole region its character. Bedrock sits close to the surface across this plain — the Garson and Tyndall quarries still cut the mottled Tyndall stone used in the Manitoba Legislature — and that shallow rock changes everything from fence posts to basements. {COMPANY_NAME} treats rock-contingency planning as standard scope in the central Interlake rather than a surprise charge. Stony Mountain's federal penitentiary, in operation since 1877, marks the tier's southern rise.

The northern tier belongs to the lakes. Gimli — heart of the historic 'New Iceland' settlement — pairs a commercial fishing fleet with the Diageo distillery that ages Crown Royal beside Lake Winnipeg; Winnipeg Beach and Dunnottar carry a century of boardwalk-resort cottages now converting to year-round homes; Arborg, Fisher and St. Laurent (a historic Métis community on Lake Manitoba) serve the farm flats. Shoreline {SERVICE} work here means wind loading, ice heave and the plumbing/heating quirks of cottage conversions.
