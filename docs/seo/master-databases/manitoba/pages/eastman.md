## About Eastman

Eastman is southeastern Manitoba — the province's fastest-growing belt, running from Winnipeg's eastern edge to the Ontario boundary. It pairs the booming Mennonite-rooted service corridor around Steinbach (population 17,806, Manitoba's third-largest city) and Niverville (+28% growth 2016–2021) with francophone parishes like St. Adolphe and St-Pierre-Jolys, commuter LUDs like Lorette and Oakbank, and the Canadian Shield cottage country of Lac du Bonnet, Pinawa and the Whiteshell. Terrain shifts from Red River clay in the west to granite and boreal forest in the east.

## Across the Eastman region

Eastman covers southeastern Manitoba from the Red River floodplain to the Ontario line, and no region in the province adds people — or new construction — faster. Steinbach (17,806) anchors the belt as Manitoba's third-largest city, its dealership-and-trucking economy built on generations of Mennonite entrepreneurship; Niverville grew 28% between the 2016 and 2021 censuses; and Hanover, Taché, Ritchot and Springfield fill in with LUD communities — Mitchell, Blumenort, Grunthal, Lorette, Landmark, Oakbank, St. Adolphe, Île-des-Chênes — that function as Winnipeg's southeastern commuter ring. For {SERVICE}, that means an unusually young housing stock: warranty-era builds where clean workmanship and documentation matter as much as the fix itself.

The ground tells two stories. West of roughly Ste. Anne, communities sit on the same expansive Lake Agassiz clay as Winnipeg, and the Red River towns — St. Adolphe most famously — live behind ring dikes that held through the 1997 Flood of the Century. {COMPANY_NAME} scopes work there for clay movement, drainage and flood-zone rules. East of the clay line, the land climbs into Canadian Shield granite and boreal forest: Lac du Bonnet, Pinawa (the planned town built for the Whiteshell Laboratories nuclear-research campus), Powerview-Pine Falls on the Winnipeg River's hydro chain, and thousands of cottages toward Whiteshell Provincial Park. Out there the questions flip — thin soils over bedrock, wells and septic instead of municipal service, and seasonal access roads.

History runs deep under the growth: the Dawson Trail — Canada's first all-Canadian route west — passes through Ste. Anne and Lorette country, and the Crow Wing ox-cart trail crosses the region's south through St-Pierre-Jolys and St. Malo, whose provincial-park beach and grotto anchor the francophone south.
