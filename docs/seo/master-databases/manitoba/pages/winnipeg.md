## About Winnipeg, Manitoba

Winnipeg is Manitoba's capital and largest city — 749,607 residents at the 2021 census, more than half the province, up 6.3% from 705,244 in 2016. It spreads 461.78 km² across the flat clay bed of glacial Lake Agassiz at the confluence of the Red and Assiniboine rivers. The economy spans aerospace (StandardAero, Magellan, Boeing), logistics (CentrePort Canada, CN Symington yard, Richardson International Airport), finance (Canada Life, IG Wealth, Wawanesa), agri-food (Richardson International), healthcare (Health Sciences Centre, St. Boniface Hospital) and CFB Winnipeg/17 Wing. Housing runs from pre-1920 brick in the Exchange District to master-planned Waverley West.
