## About Transcona, Manitoba

Transcona is a district of about 36,280 people in far east Winnipeg. Founded as a railway town — its name blends 'Transcontinental' and 'Strathcona' — it grew up around the Canadian National Transcona Shops, one of the largest railway-car repair complexes in the country, and was a separate town until amalgamation in 1972. It keeps its own main street along Regent Avenue and its own identity, mixing early-1900s railway-era homes, postwar bungalows, and newer east-end subdivisions.
