## About St. Laurent, Manitoba

The Rural Municipality of St. Laurent is a growing municipality of 1,542 people (2021 census) on the east shore of Lake Manitoba, 69 km northwest of Winnipeg — Manitoba's largest Métis community, with deep Michif and French-Métis heritage. A fishing and lakeshore community, much of its low-lying land was hit hard by the 2011 Lake Manitoba flood, the costliest overland flood in provincial history. Its Métis heritage, its Lake Manitoba shore, and its flood-zone realities define its market.
