## About Westman

Westman is southwestern Manitoba, centred on Brandon (51,313) — the province's second city, the 'Wheat City' — with its Maple Leaf Foods pork plant, Brandon University and Assiniboine College, and the Royal Manitoba Winter Fair. The region spans the broad Assiniboine River valley, the Carberry sandhills beside Spruce Woods Provincial Park, Virden's oil patch on the Williston Basin, CFB Shilo's artillery base, Neepawa's lily-and-pork economy, and farm towns to the Saskatchewan and U.S. lines — on sandier, better-drained soils than the Red River Valley.

## Across the Westman region

Westman is Manitoba's southwest quarter, and Brandon — the Wheat City, 51,313 people in the broad Assiniboine River valley — is its unmistakable capital. The city pairs one of Canada's largest pork plants (Maple Leaf Foods) with Brandon University, Assiniboine College and the Royal Manitoba Winter Fair, and its housing runs from Victorian brick off Rosser Avenue through postwar wards to the fast-building south end above the valley. For {SERVICE}, Brandon's valley setting is the first consideration: slopes, springs and the flood memory of 2011, when the Assiniboine tested the 18th Street dikes, shape how {COMPANY_NAME} scopes valley-floor and brow-of-hill properties differently in this part of Manitoba.

The region around it is a study in prairie variety. East along the Trans-Canada, Carberry's potato country meets the Carberry sandhills, where Spruce Woods Provincial Park's Spirit Sands hold open dunes unique on the Canadian prairie — and where CFB Shilo's artillery ranges make the base community a steady, specialized market. North on Highway 10, Minnedosa terraces down its namesake valley to the lake, and Neepawa — Margaret Laurence's hometown and the self-declared Lily Capital of the World — has boomed on the HyLife pork plant's workforce. West along Highway 1, Virden has pumped oil from the Williston Basin since the 1950s, its sandstone-trimmed downtown a legacy of an earlier boom; Pipestone, Sifton and Melita carry the farm economy to the corners, and Sioux Valley Dakota Nation sits on the Assiniboine west of Brandon. To the south, Killarney-Turtle Mountain wraps a lake-in-town resort feel against the forested Turtle Mountain plateau and the International Peace Garden on the U.S. line.
