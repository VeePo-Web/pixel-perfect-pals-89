## About Brandon, Manitoba

Brandon is Manitoba's second-largest city, with 51,313 residents at the 2021 census, up 5% from 48,883 in 2016. Founded as a Canadian Pacific Railway townsite in the early 1880s and nicknamed the Wheat City, it sits in the Assiniboine River valley 202 km west of Winnipeg on the Trans-Canada Highway. Its economy rests on agriculture, the Maple Leaf Foods pork plant, Brandon University, Assiniboine College and regional health care, and the Keystone Centre hosts the Royal Manitoba Winter Fair. Housing spans a Victorian-Edwardian core near Rosser Avenue, postwar wards and growing south-end suburbs.
