## About Flin Flon, Manitoba

Flin Flon is a mining city on the Manitoba–Saskatchewan border; its Manitoba portion had a 2021 census population of 4,940. Copper and zinc built it — Hudbay's operations gave the city its shape — and it is famous for houses anchored to bare Canadian Shield granite, many without basements because there is no soil to dig. Named for the pulp-novel character Josiah Flintabbatey Flonatin, it sits 630 km northwest of Winnipeg amid Shield rock, boreal forest, and lakes, with the boundary between the two provinces running through town.
