## Saskatoon Region — slug `saskatoon-region`
- **Climate:** Dfb; −30 °C winter design lows, ~340–360 mm precipitation, strong freeze–thaw shoulders;
  South Saskatchewan River moderates the valley corridor.
- **Geology/soil:** dark-brown chernozem grading to black soil northeast; river-valley slopes and
  sand-loam pockets along the South Saskatchewan.
- **Economy:** Saskatoon anchors mining head offices (Nutrien, Cameco), the University of Saskatchewan
  and Innovation Place; three working potash mines ring the region (Nutrien Allan, Cory at Vanscoy,
  Colonsay); grain and pulse farming across every RM; Warman and Martensville are among Canada's
  fastest-growing cities as commuter satellites.
- **Building stock:** pre-war brick core neighbourhoods in Saskatoon; large 2000s–2020s suburban tracts
  in Warman, Martensville and Saskatoon's edge wards; acreage subdivisions across the RM of Corman Park
  (Saskatchewan's most populous RM); Mennonite-heritage farmyards on the Hague–Osler corridor.
- **Landmarks:** South Saskatchewan River & Meewasin Valley; Wanuskewin Heritage Park; University of
  Saskatchewan campus; Highway 11 (Louis Riel Trail), Highway 16 (Yellowhead) and Highway 7 corridors;
  CFB Dundurn military detachment south of the city.

## Regina Region — slug `regina-region`
- **Climate:** Dfb, drier than Saskatoon (~300–390 mm); notorious freeze–thaw and wind exposure on the
  open Regina Plains.
- **Geology/soil:** heavy lacustrine "Regina clay" (expansive gumbo) across the plains — the defining
  regional condition for anything touching foundations, grading or drainage; Qu'Appelle Valley cuts a
  glacial spillway north of the city.
- **Economy:** provincial capital economy (Legislature, SaskPower, SaskTel, SGI head offices), EVRAZ
  steel, the Co-op Refinery Complex, Viterra and Brandt; durum, lentil and canola farming on the plains;
  Highway 1 East commuter corridor (White City, Pilot Butte, Balgonie, Edenwold RM) is among the
  province's fastest-growing.
- **Building stock:** century-old Cathedral and Crescents character homes on clay; post-war bungalow
  belts; new suburbs (Harbour Landing, The Creeks) and acreage-estate growth in Edenwold and Sherwood
  RMs; lake cottages at Regina Beach and Buena Vista.
- **Landmarks:** Saskatchewan Legislative Building & Wascana Centre; RCMP Academy "Depot" Division;
  Mosaic Stadium; Qu'Appelle Valley & Last Mountain Lake (Regina Beach); Highways 1, 6, 11 and 39.
- **Region page:** {COMPANY_NAME} across Regina, the Highway 1 East bedroom towns (White City, Pilot
  Butte, Balgonie, Qu'Appelle), the valley towns (Lumsden, Regina Beach, Buena Vista, Bethune, Southey)
  and the plains RMs (Sherwood, Edenwold, Lumsden, Lajord, Francis, Pense, Cupar, South Qu'Appelle,
  Longlaketon, Bratt's Lake, Redburn) plus Milestone and Rouleau on the Highway 39 CanAm corridor.

## Central Saskatchewan — slug `central-saskatchewan`
- **Climate:** parkland Dfb — snowier winters than the south plains, black-soil moisture, aspen bluffs;
  freeze–thaw plus heavier snow-load design.
- **Geology/soil:** black chernozem parkland (some of Canada's most productive grain land); glacial till
  knolls; South Saskatchewan and North Saskatchewan river valleys converge east of Prince Albert.
- **Economy:** Prince Albert is the "Gateway to the North" service hub (forestry, health care,
  corrections); Humboldt anchors the German-Catholic St. Peter's Colony farm belt with BHP's Jansen
  potash project and Nutrien Lanigan nearby; Melfort sits on deep black Carrot River loam; potash
  (Lanigan, Jansen), grain, livestock and mixed farms everywhere; Watrous–Manitou Beach adds resort
  tourism on saline Little Manitou Lake.
- **Building stock:** early-1900s rail-town main streets (Rosthern, Duck Lake, Wakaw); farmyards and
  grain-belt bungalows; Prince Albert's mixed urban stock; lakefront cottages toward Tobin Lake
  (Nipawin) and Manitou Beach.
- **Landmarks:** Batoche National Historic Site and the 1885 Duck Lake battlefield; Fort Carlton; Seager
  Wheeler's Maple Grove Farm NHS (Rosthern); Little Manitou Lake & Danceland (Watrous); Tobin Lake
  walleye fishery (Nipawin); Highways 2, 3, 5, 11 and 41.
- **Region page:** Prince Albert, Humboldt, Melfort, Tisdale, Nipawin, Rosthern, Watrous, Lanigan,
  Davidson, Shellbrook, Wynyard, Birch Hills, Wakaw, Strasbourg, Cudworth, Wadena, Bruno, Duck Lake,
  Blaine Lake, Raymore, St. Brieux plus the black-soil RMs (Prince Albert, Buckland, St. Louis, Duck
  Lake, Shellbrook, Canwood, Paddockwood, Torch River, Hoodoo, Leask, Humboldt, Three Lakes).

## Southeast Saskatchewan — slug `southeast-saskatchewan`
- **Climate:** Dfb; Estevan records more sunshine than any other Canadian city (~2,500+ hours); hot
  summers, hail exposure, freeze–thaw.
- **Geology/soil:** dark-brown to black transition; Bakken oil formation underlies the far southeast;
  the Qu'Appelle Valley lakes chain (Echo, Mission, Katepwa) crosses the north of the region; Moose
  Mountain uplands rise near Carlyle.
- **Economy:** potash superlatives — Mosaic Esterhazy K1/K2/K3 is the world's largest potash operation
  and Nutrien Rocanville (near Moosomin) among the largest; Bakken oil (Estevan, Weyburn, Carlyle,
  Oxbow, Redvers); Estevan's power plants including Boundary Dam — site of the world's first commercial
  carbon-capture coal unit; Yorkton's canola-crush plants; CN rail heritage in Melville (Saskatchewan's
  smallest city); Ukrainian and Doukhobor farm heritage around Canora and Kamsack.
- **Building stock:** oil-boom 2010s builds in Estevan/Weyburn; rail-era main streets (Moosomin,
  Grenfell, Wolseley); mine-workforce housing (Esterhazy, Moosomin); valley cottages at Fort Qu'Appelle;
  farmsteads across the plains RMs.
- **Landmarks:** Qu'Appelle Valley & Treaty 4 grounds (Fort Qu'Appelle, 1874); Motherwell Homestead NHS
  (Abernethy); Yorkton Film Festival (North America's longest-running); Moose Mountain Provincial Park;
  Highway 1 border corridor and Highway 39 CanAm corridor to the North Portal border crossing.
- **Region page:** Yorkton, Weyburn, Estevan, Melville, Fort Qu'Appelle, Indian Head, Esterhazy,
  Moosomin, Canora, Kamsack, Carlyle, Grenfell, Kipling, Foam Lake, Preeceville, Oxbow, Redvers,
  Langenburg, Wolseley, Balcarres, Ituna, Cupar, Radville, Yellow Grass, Ogema plus the RMs (Weyburn,
  Estevan, Orkney, North Qu'Appelle, Indian Head, Montmartre, Abernethy, Lipton, Cana, Caledonia,
  Stanley, Kellross).

## Southern Saskatchewan — slug `southern-saskatchewan`
- **Climate:** drier south-central plains (~330 mm), long sunshine hours, chinook-tinged mild spells,
  hard freeze–thaw swings.
- **Geology/soil:** dark-brown chernozem; the hummocky Missouri Coteau ridge crosses the region.
- **Economy:** Moose Jaw pairs 15 Wing (home of the Snowbirds) with agri-processing and a
  tourism-forward historic downtown; Briercrest College effectively IS the village of Caronport;
  Assiniboia hubs the durum/lentil south; Highway 1/2/39 junction logistics.
- **Building stock:** Moose Jaw's heritage brick downtown and post-war bungalows; college housing in
  Caronport; farm-service-town stock in Assiniboia; McKillop's Last Mountain Lake cottage strip.
- **Landmarks:** Tunnels of Moose Jaw; Mac the Moose (world's largest moose statue); 15 Wing Moose Jaw /
  Snowbirds; Temple Gardens Mineral Spa; Missouri Coteau.
- **Region page:** Moose Jaw, Assiniboia, Caronport and the RMs of Moose Jaw, Baildon, Caron, Dufferin,
  Marquis, Sarnia and McKillop (Last Mountain Lake east shore).

## Southwest Saskatchewan — slug `southwest-saskatchewan`
- **Climate:** the driest, sunniest corner of the province (Palliser Triangle, ~300–330 mm), true
  chinook influence, biggest wind exposure.
- **Geology/soil:** brown chernozem shortgrass prairie; Swift Current Creek valley.
- **Economy:** Swift Current is the retail/agri hub of the entire southwest (durum, lentils, ranching,
  wind power); Gravelbourg carries Saskatchewan's francophone heritage.
- **Building stock:** Swift Current's mix of heritage core and steady new subdivisions; ranch yards and
  farm-town stock elsewhere.
- **Landmarks:** Swift Current Creek & Chinook Pathway; Our Lady of Assumption Co-Cathedral
  (Gravelbourg); Trans-Canada Highway 1 and Highway 4 junction.
- **Region page:** Swift Current, Gravelbourg and the RM of Swift Current, with the deferred southwest
  (Maple Creek, Shaunavon, Leader, Gull Lake, Eastend, Ponteix, Kyle, Lafleche) named as service-area

## West Central Saskatchewan — slug `west-central-saskatchewan`
- **Climate:** dry dark-brown belt (~330 mm), high sunshine, strong winds across open wheat country.
- **Geology/soil:** dark-brown chernozem wheat land; Lake Diefenbaker reservoir and the Gardiner Dam
  (one of the world's largest embankment dams) define the southeast edge.
- **Economy:** Kindersley anchors Viking-formation oil and gas; Rosetown sits at the heart of the wheat
  belt (Highways 7 × 4); Outlook is Saskatchewan's irrigation capital drawing on Lake Diefenbaker;
  Biggar — "New York is big, but this is Biggar" — mixes rail heritage with grain.
- **Building stock:** oilfield-era housing in Kindersley; irrigation-farm yards around Outlook;
  rail-town main streets in Biggar and Rosetown.
- **Landmarks:** Gardiner Dam & Lake Diefenbaker; Highway 7 (Saskatoon–Calgary corridor); Biggar's
  famous town slogan sign.
- **Region page:** Kindersley, Rosetown, Outlook, Biggar and the RMs (Kindersley, Biggar, Eagle Creek,
  Fertile Valley, Rudy, St. Andrews, Loreburn, Marriott).

## Northwest Saskatchewan — slug `northwest-saskatchewan`
- **Climate:** parkland-to-boreal transition; heavier snow, −30s winters; North Saskatchewan River
  valley corridor.
- **Geology/soil:** black chernozem parkland grading to grey wooded soils and boreal forest north of
  Meadow Lake.
- **Economy:** the Battlefords are the trading hub of the northwest (health care, retail, agriculture);
  Lloydminster — the Border City astride the Alberta boundary on the 4th meridian — runs on heavy oil
  and its upgrader; Meadow Lake pairs forestry mills with gateway tourism to Meadow Lake Provincial
  Park; Unity produces salt; Macklin is the self-declared world capital of the game of bunnock.
- **Building stock:** North Battleford/Battleford twin-town mix of heritage and post-war stock; oilpatch
  housing in Lloydminster; lakeland cottages (Meota, Mervin RM: Turtle Lake); farm belt bungalows on the
  Yellowhead.
- **Landmarks:** Fort Battleford NHS (Battleford was capital of the North-West Territories, 1876–1883);
  the 4th-meridian border markers in Lloydminster; Meadow Lake Provincial Park; Yellowhead Highway 16;
  Macklin's giant bunnock monument.
- **Region page:** North Battleford, Battleford, Lloydminster, Meadow Lake, Unity, Wilkie, Maidstone,
  Macklin, Spiritwood, Hafford plus the RMs (Battle River, North Battleford, Meota, Mervin, Britannia,
  Wilton, Meadow Lake, Spiritwood, Redberry, Mayfield, Douglas).
