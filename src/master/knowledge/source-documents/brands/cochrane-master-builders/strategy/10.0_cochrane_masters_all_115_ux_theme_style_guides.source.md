| 0 | CochraneMasterBuilders.ca | Cochrane Master Builders | Umbrella / Authority | UX Theme Style Guide: Master Builders — Master trust hub for Master Builders..  Brand Feel: clean, simple, deep, local, family-owned, and premium without being flashy. The page should feel like a specialist service brand operating under one dependable Cochrane Masters standard. For Cochrane Master Builders, the first impression should be: “these people understand this exact problem, they can explain it clearly, and they will not cut corners.”  Colour + Material Direction: heritage forest, warm limestone, soft ivory, charcoal, brushed brass. Use the lightest colour for whitespace and breathing room, the darkest tone for trust-heavy headings, and the strongest accent only for CTA buttons, selected states, pricing highlights, proof badges, and process markers. Keep colour restrained so the site feels high-end and fast instead of loud.  Layout System: Hero with one clear promise → service selector → simple process → proof and FAQs → pricing guidance → one strong inquiry CTA. Keep each section short on mobile, with one clear purpose per screen. Use sticky mobile CTA, fixed-height cards, compact FAQ accordions, and service-specific trust blocks. The structure should reduce anxiety before it asks for contact information.  Bespoke Components: 1) Service ecosystem map with all Masters brands grouped by home system. 2) One inquiry path that routes users by project type and budget. 3) Neighbour proof strip with local service area cards. 4) Long-term family-building manifesto section.  Style Rules: use large calm headings, tight subheads, strong spacing, real project photography, tactile close-ups, simple icons, and thin dividers. For Master Builders, prioritize visuals that show the actual craft, the before/after transformation, or the hidden system that protects the home long term.  Motion + Interaction: use subtle TikTok-level pacing—quick, satisfying, easy to scan—but keep it Fantasy-level refined. Motion should explain process, not decorate the page. Use soft reveals, small hover states, swipeable cards, and comparison sliders only where they improve understanding. No heavy parallax, no autoplay video, and no gimmicky animations.  UX Keywords: authority, trust hub, central navigation, family standard, service ecosystem, calm control centre, strong foundations, neighbour trust, built for those who come after us, clear estimate, photo-first intake, practical premium, fast load, low cognitive load.  Performance Direction: build mostly static, keep hero media compressed, lazy-load galleries, reserve image dimensions, avoid animation libraries, pre-render FAQ/schema, keep forms short, and target a page that feels instant on mobile. Premium should come from speed, clarity, spacing, and confidence. |
