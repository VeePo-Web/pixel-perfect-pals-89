## Sheet: 04 Domain Rules

| Rule | Use This | Avoid This | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary pattern | Cochrane + Service + Masters + .ca | Random names that do not include Cochrane or the service keyword | Keeps domains obvious, local, and keyword-aligned. |
| Spelling | CochraneCommercialMasters.ca | CochraneCommericalMasters.ca | Correct spelling improves trust and prevents wasted domains. |
| Canadian wording | CochraneLabourMasters.ca | CochraneLaborMasters.ca as the main public brand | Canada-facing spelling feels more local; buy Labor only as a redirect if desired. |
| Service nouns | CochraneFlooringMasters.ca | CochraneFloorMasters.ca | Users usually search 'flooring contractor,' not 'floor contractor.' |
| Landscape wording | CochraneLandscapingMasters.ca | CochraneLandScapeMasters.ca | 'Landscaping' is the service search term and removes odd capitalization. |
| Domain length | Use the shortest clear service name that still matches intent | Overly long domains for small subservices | Long domains are harder to remember and type. |
| Broad verticals | Use Construction/Renovation as pillar sites only | Duplicating the same content across every broad site | Controls cannibalization and quality risk. |
| Licensed work | Use 'via licensed partners' where needed | Making direct license claims before operators are confirmed | Protects trust and compliance. |
| Availability | Verify before purchase | Assuming domains are available | Live availability was not checked in this session. |
| Content quality | Each domain gets unique proof, pricing, FAQs, schema, and local content | Thin copied sites with swapped service words | Protects SEO quality and conversion performance. |
| Email routing | Inquiry@CochraneMasterBuilders.ca | Separate unmanaged inboxes for every site | Centralizes lead intake and follow-up. |
| Tracking | Use domain-specific labels/UTMs | No analytics separation | Shows which exact service domain creates leads. |
