## Document category
`brands/cochrane-master-builders/` → `strategy/` → pricing & services catalog (join-companion to v1.0 SEO Master Plan)

## Main purpose
Canonical operational catalog of **what each Cochrane Master Builders site sells, at what mid-high CAD price ranges, and under what compliance guardrails.** It is the join-table partner to the v1.0 family-of-sites Master Plan: identical row count (~116), joinable on the `Master Row` key, identical `Recommended Domain`, `Website / Brand`, and `Cluster` columns. Together the two files form the complete remix base for every planned spin-off site.

## What this document should influence
- Service-page content on each spin-off site (the bulleted services list).
- Pricing UI: ranges, "From $X CAD" framing, compliance footnotes.
- Compliance / disclaimer copy (the `Pricing Notes / Compliance Guardrails` column is the source of truth for what may and may not be claimed — scope, licensing, third-party, audit caveats).
- Quote / inquiry / intake form scoping (typical job size shapes which fields to ask and budget min/max).
- Lead-qualification logic (price floor/ceiling sets the qualification threshold).
- "Get a quote" CTA copy and microcopy on each spin-off.
- Cross-sell and cluster bundling (the `Cluster` column joins to Master Plan sheet `02 Service Clusters`).

## What kinds of future prompts or decisions should trigger consulting this document
Any prompt mentioning: *price, pricing, "how much," cost, range, quote, estimate, disclaimer, compliance, scope, what does X site offer, service list, service catalog, typical job size, qualify the lead, pricing UI, tier, "from $", CAD, guardrail, what we can/can't claim, what's included, mid-high pricing, audited.*

## Which parts of the website / system it applies to
Every Cochrane Master Builders **family-of-sites spin-off** (115+ sites) — anywhere a price, service list, scope statement, disclaimer, or quote-form lives. Also the Cochrane hub when it surfaces aggregated pricing or "what we do" copy.

## How it should guide output quality
- Pricing must always read as **mid-high CAD ranges**, never as a fixed quote.
- Disclaimers and scope statements must be drawn from this row's `Pricing Notes / Compliance Guardrails` — not invented.
- Service lists on a spin-off site must mirror the bulleted services in this row's `Detailed Services & Mid-High Pricing (CAD)` column.
- Currency must always be displayed as `CAD` (or `$X CAD`) — never bare dollars.
- Pricing should be surfaced as "indicative, audited snapshot" — never a binding quote.

## Adaptation rules / firewall
- **Brand context is Cochrane Master Builders**, not Masters Concierge or VeePo. Do **not** apply the automotive vocabulary firewall here.
- **Compliance Guardrails are binding.** If the column says something must be disclosed, scoped, or NOT claimed, that rule wins. Conflicts surface as a memory-conflict flag — never silent overrides.
- **Join discipline.** Every reference to a site's pricing must use the same `Master Row` value it has in the v1.0 Master Plan. If a row appears here but is missing in the Master Plan (or vice versa), surface a **Sync Discrepancy** flag — both files must move together.
- **Cluster + Brand + Domain must match** the v1.0 Master Plan exactly for the same `Master Row`. Any drift is a bug to surface, not auto-correct.
- **Live `.ca` availability NOT verified** in either file — inherit the Master Plan's WHOIS-check caveat.

## How it relates to brand identity, ICP, UX standards, visual polish, conversion, component decisions
- **Brand identity:** Cochrane "Family Legacy Standard" / *Foundations For Generations After Us* tone wraps every price + disclaimer surfacing. Pricing tone stays measured, generational, never discount-driven.
- **ICP:** Subcontractor / Mothers / Grandfathers ICP docs determine *how* a price range is framed (trust language for Mothers, scope clarity for Grandfathers, transactional clarity for Subcontractors).
- **UX standards:** Pricing UI inherits the Cochrane bespoke / traditional UX phase report — calm, declarative, no sales-pressure patterns.
- **Conversion:** Quote-form scope + CTA copy use the typical-job-size language to qualify leads early without feeling transactional.

## Mode-OS pairing
- **Architect** uses this as the *offer + pricing data model* when designing pricing pages, service grids, quote forms, or intake flows for a spin-off.
- **Mapper** renders per-cluster pricing-tier comparison diagrams or per-site offer maps from the Cluster + Master Row joins.
- **Auditor** pressure-tests every generated pricing UI or copy against (a) the row's Compliance Guardrails and (b) the Master Plan join (row count, Recommended Domain, Website/Brand, Cluster). Any mismatch = **Critical** bug.

## Practical examples of when to use it
- *"Build a pricing section for the Basement Suite Masters site."* → Look up that brand's row; render `Detailed Services` as the catalog, the price ranges as "From $X CAD" rows, and `Pricing Notes` verbatim as compliance footnotes below the table.
- *"Write the disclaimer under the price."* → Pull language from the `Pricing Notes / Compliance Guardrails` column for that row.
- *"What should the quote-form scope ask?"* → Use the typical-job-size language to set min/max budget fields and required scope inputs.
- *"Recommend a bundled package."* → Group rows by `Cluster` and surface the highest-value offers in that cluster, anchored to the Master Plan's cluster definition.
- *"Is this site allowed to claim X?"* → Check this row's Compliance Guardrails *before* any copy ships.
