## 8. Rules — non-negotiables this doc enforces

1. **1.2 supersedes 1.0 on positioning conflicts.** Always check both; when they disagree, follow 1.2 and note the delta in the plan.
2. **Differentiation must be specific.** Generic claims ("quality you can trust") fail. State the specific axis CMB outperforms on, with proof. (source: competitive-positioning section)
3. **Pricing posture is a brand decision, not a page decision.** Whatever 1.2 says about transparency applies to every surface (home, services, area pages, FAQ) — no per-page exceptions. (source: pricing posture)
4. **Page pillars before sub-sections.** A topic earns top-level status only if 1.2 names it as a pillar. Sub-pillars live as sections, not nav items. (source: UX implications)
5. **Refined hero promise must trace to 1.2's pitch.** Second-draft hero copy cites the section in this source it derives from. (source: executive summary delta)
6. **Never out-message a competitor by sounding like them.** Differentiation never adopts a competitor's slogan structure or palette logic. (source: competitive-positioning + cross-link to `gr-zero-sister-fingerprints`)
7. **Pricing disclosure has no "marketing exception."** If 1.2 mandates visible ranges, do not hide them behind a CTA, modal, or "request a quote" gate.
8. **Differentiation claims must survive the legal/trust audit.** Anything claimed here flows into `LEGAL_TRUST_GUIDE.md` for substantiation.
9. **Strategic edits to this layer require operator sign-off.** The AI may propose strategic refinements, but never ships them silently into production copy.
10. **Cite 1.2 by name in the deep plan.** When this doc shapes a decision, the plan's Brand-truth refs section names the source path and the section consulted (per `PLAN_FIRST_DISCIPLINE.md` §2).

## 9. Anti-patterns

- A hero pitch identical to a competitor's, justified as "tested." Violates Rules 2 + 6.
- Pricing visible on the home page but hidden on service pages. Violates Rule 3.
- "Why Choose Us" bullets that could appear on any builder's site. Violates Rule 2.
- A new top-level nav item with no pillar status in 1.2. Violates Rule 4.
- A "starting at $X" headline with no substantiation in the warranty/legal layer. Violates Rule 8.

## 10. Worked example

**Trigger.** Request: "Add a 'Custom Homes from $X' badge to the hero."

**Step 1 — recognize.** Pricing transparency + differentiation triggers → this doc.

**Step 2 — read source.** Parse PDF. Read pricing posture + competitive-positioning sections.

**Step 3 — apply rules.** Rule 3 says pricing posture is brand-wide; Rule 8 says any number must survive substantiation; Rule 7 forbids gating; Rule 2 says claims must be specific.

**Step 4 — decision.** If 1.2 mandates transparent ranges, the badge ships with a real range (not "starting at"), with the inclusions/exclusions in a footnote linked to a substantiation page. If 1.2 mandates opacity, no badge — link to a "How we price" page instead. Either way, Rule 6 (no competitor mimicry) governs the visual treatment.

**Step 5 — verify.** Plan §12 lists `gr-real-business-signals` and `gr-legal-pages-bespoke` for the substantiation footnote.
