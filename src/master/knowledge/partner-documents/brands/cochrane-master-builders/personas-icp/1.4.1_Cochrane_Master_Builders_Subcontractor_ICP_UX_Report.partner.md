## 2. What this document is

The subcontractor ideal-customer profile — who CMB wants to partner with on the trade side (framers, electricians, plumbers, roofers, drywallers, etc.), what they care about, what they fear, what makes them say yes to a partnership, and the UX implications for any partner-facing flow (signup, vetting, scheduling, payment expectations).

## 8. Rules — non-negotiables this doc enforces

1. **Respect their time.** Every vendor-facing screen targets ≤ 90 seconds of attention. Forms summarize remaining steps + estimated time at the top. (source: UX implications)
2. **Plain trade language, never marketing fluff.** "Send us your WCB and license" beats "We'd love to learn more about your professional credentials." (source: copy register)
3. **No homeowner-facing trust signals on vendor pages.** Star ratings and "5-star reviewed" mean nothing here. Vendors care about payment terms, schedule reliability, and project pipeline. (source: trust section)
4. **Payment expectations stated up front.** Net terms, deposit policy, and payment cadence appear before the vendor commits to anything. (source: trust section)
5. **License / insurance upload uses native file pickers, not branded widgets.** Tradespeople upload from a phone on a job site; nothing fancy. (source: UX implications)
6. **Mobile-first vendor flows.** Assume the vendor is on a phone with one hand, possibly with gloves. Touch targets ≥ 48×48. (source: UX implications)
7. **No homeowner empathy language on vendor surfaces.** "We know how stressful this is" reads patronizing to a tradesperson. (source: copy register)
8. **Project-pipeline framing wins over brand-prestige framing.** Vendors say yes for steady work, not for the logo. Lead with pipeline + payment + clarity. (source: ICP profile)
9. **Vendor email subject lines are operational, not promotional.** "Your WCB expires in 30 days" beats "Important update from CMB!" (source: notification section)
10. **Brand voice still applies, in adapted form.** Heritage register lives — it just speaks plainly about work, money, and reliability. (cross-link to `brand-identity/1.2.1` Rule 10)

## 9. Anti-patterns

- Vendor application with a 14-field one-page form. Violates Rule 1.
- "Join our family of trusted partners and unlock unlimited opportunities!" Violates Rules 2 + 7.
- Vendor page leading with homeowner reviews. Violates Rule 3.
- Payment terms "discussed after onboarding." Violates Rule 4.
- A custom-styled drag-and-drop with a hover state for the license upload. Violates Rule 5.
- Vendor portal designed desktop-first with a mobile afterthought. Violates Rule 6.
- "We know your time is precious — that's why we built this beautiful experience for you 💙" Violates Rules 2 + 7 + `brand-identity/1.2.1` (emoji ban).
- Email subject "🎉 You're invited to join the CMB family!" Violates Rules 9 + `brand-identity/1.2.1` Rule 7.

## 10. Worked example

**Trigger.** Request: "Build the `/partners/apply` page."

**Step 1 — recognize.** Vendor signup → this doc primary; `ux-design/1.3.1` for shell; `brand-identity/1.2.1` for voice.

**Step 2 — read source.** Parse DOCX. Read ICP profile + UX implications + copy register + trust section.

**Step 3 — apply rules.** Rule 8: hero leads with project pipeline + payment clarity, not legacy prose. Rule 4: payment terms visible before form. Rule 1: form summarizes steps + time. Rule 5: native file inputs. Rule 6: mobile-first, 48×48 targets. Rule 2: plain trade language. Rule 10: still in heritage register, just plainspoken.

**Step 4 — decision.** Page structure: hero with pipeline + payment terms + "what we ask for" clarity → multi-step form (≤ 4 fields/step) with native uploads → confirmation with what-happens-next timeline. Vendor email confirmation is operational subject line.

**Step 5 — verify.** Plan §12 lists `gr-booking-one-tap` (vendor form ≤ 90s), `gr-wcag-aa` (touch targets), `gr-real-business-signals` (payment terms upfront), `gr-bespoke-style-guide-live` (vendor surfaces still match the bespoke style guide).
