## 2. What this document is

The mother-persona ICP — the family decision-maker (often but not exclusively the mother) researching builders, comparing trust signals, evaluating safety of contractors entering the home, scheduling around kids and routines, and weighing pricing transparency against perceived risk. Includes the UX implications: how she scans, what makes her bounce, what makes her book, and how she wants to be talked to.

## 8. Rules — non-negotiables this doc enforces

1. **No surprise costs.** Pricing context (range + inclusions/exclusions) appears before any CTA she might tap. (source: pricing posture)
2. **Safety signals are concrete.** "Background-checked, insured, named project lead, daily cleanup" beats "trusted professionals." (source: safety section)
3. **Scheduling respects family rhythms.** Time windows offered match school-run / nap / dinner realities; never just "9–5." (source: scheduling section)
4. **Reviews lead with family-relevant voices.** Testimonials selected for this persona mention kids, pets, mess management, communication — not just "great work." (source: trust selection)
5. **Communication cadence promised, in writing.** "You'll hear from us within X hours; daily updates during work" is part of the trust package. (source: trust section)
6. **No high-pressure CTAs.** "Book now or lose your spot" is forbidden. Confidence reads safer than urgency. (source: copy register)
7. **Mobile late-night reading mode.** Copy scans at 9pm on a phone in bed: short paragraphs, scannable headings, no walls of text. (source: UX implications)
8. **Objection handling is explicit, not hidden in FAQ.** Top 3 mother-persona objections (mess, disruption, contractors-in-the-home) get addressed in body, not buried. (source: copy register + cross-link to `src/config/fear-dispel.ts`)
9. **Trust micro-copy near every CTA.** Insurance + warranty mention sits within thumb-distance of the booking button. (source: trust section + cross-link to `gr-real-business-signals`)
10. **Photography never includes people.** Reinforces master image rule. Real project photography (rooms, finishes, before/after) wins. (cross-link to `ux-design/1.3.1` Rule 13 + memory)
11. **Reassurance, not condescension.** "We've got you" not "We know how hard this is for you." (source: copy register)
12. **Form fields prioritize her information shortcuts.** Photo upload, address autocomplete, calendar with pre-blocked school hours. (source: UX implications)

## 9. Anti-patterns

- Hero CTA "Book now — only 2 spots left this month!" Violates Rule 6.
- "Starting at $X" with no inclusions list within 1 scroll. Violates Rule 1.
- "Our team are trusted professionals you can count on." Violates Rule 2 (vague).
- Booking calendar offering only 9am–5pm windows. Violates Rule 3.
- Testimonial carousel of "Loved the work — Bob" / "Great team — Susan." Violates Rule 4.
- 600-word block paragraph on the home page. Violates Rule 7.
- Mess + disruption objections only addressed on the FAQ page. Violates Rule 8.
- CTA with no nearby trust micro-copy. Violates Rule 9.
- Hero photo of a smiling family with a contractor. Violates Rule 10.
- "We know how stressful renovations are for busy moms like you." Violates Rule 11.

## 10. Worked example

**Trigger.** Request: "Write the home-page service grid copy for the mother-persona-primary site."

**Step 1 — recognize.** Service grid for family decision-maker → this doc primary; `brand-identity/1.2.2` for voice; `ux-design/1.3.1` for grid composition; `seo-research/1.1` for keyword skeleton.

**Step 2 — read source.** Parse DOCX. Read copy register + objection handling + pricing posture.

**Step 3 — apply rules.** Rule 1: each service tile carries a price range or "from" with one-line inclusion. Rule 2: insured + named lead surfaced near the grid. Rule 8: top objection (e.g. "mess in the house") addressed in micro-copy under the service title. Rule 7: scannable, ≤ 25-word descriptions. Rule 11: reassurance tone.

**Step 4 — decision.** Each service tile = title (keyword-aligned per SEO), one benefit-led sentence (mother-relevant, e.g. "minimal disruption to the family routine"), price range, trust micro-copy, single CTA. Grid scannable in 6 seconds on mobile.

**Step 5 — verify.** Plan §12 lists `gr-real-business-signals`, `gr-booking-one-tap`, `gr-anti-paraphrase-readability`, `gr-wcag-aa`.
