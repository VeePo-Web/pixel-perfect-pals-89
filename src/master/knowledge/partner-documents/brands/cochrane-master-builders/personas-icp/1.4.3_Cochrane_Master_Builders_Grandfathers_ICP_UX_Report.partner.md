## 2. What this document is

The grandfather-persona ICP — the older homeowner (often a grandfather or long-tenured family patriarch) who values longevity, plainspoken honesty, demonstrated craft, in-person trust, larger touch targets, and language that respects experience without condescending. Often deciding on behalf of a multi-generational household or legacy property. Includes UX implications: type size, density, motion restraint, contact options, trust framing.

## 8. Rules — non-negotiables this doc enforces

1. **Body type starts at 18px on this persona's primary surfaces.** Never below. (source: accessibility section)
2. **Touch targets ≥ 48×48 CSS px.** Always. (source: accessibility section)
3. **Phone number visible above the fold on every page.** Tap-to-call linked. (source: contact preferences)
4. **Plainspoken copy — no jargon, no clever wordplay, no metaphors that rely on cultural references.** "We rebuild foundations" beats "We turn the page on tired structures." (source: copy register)
5. **Trust weighted toward longevity.** Years in business, founder presence, license duration, in-person credentials. Star ratings rank below these. (source: trust section)
6. **Motion restraint.** Reduced-motion respected always; default motion durations dialed back; no parallax on text. (source: UX implications + cross-link to `gr-motion-system-pinned`)
7. **High contrast mandatory.** ≥ 7:1 on body text where feasible; never below 4.5:1. (source: accessibility section + `gr-wcag-aa`)
8. **Hand-shake language allowed.** "We'll come by, walk it with you, write it up" reads trustworthy here. (source: copy register)
9. **Legal / warranty pages get bespoke prose, not legalese boilerplate.** This persona reads them. Plain language summary at top, full text below. (cross-link to `gr-legal-pages-bespoke`)
10. **Founder presence visible.** Photo (no faces of others), name, signature, years in trade — somewhere within 2 clicks of the home page. (source: trust section)
11. **No "AI-powered," "smart," "instant" messaging.** Reads as gimmick to this persona. (source: copy register)
12. **Forms are short, with phone-call alternative offered prominently.** "Or call \[number\] — we'll write it down for you." (source: form section)

## 9. Anti-patterns

- Body type at 14px on a service page. Violates Rule 1.
- 38×38 phone-link in the footer only. Violates Rules 2 + 3.
- Hero "We're disrupting the home-build experience with AI-driven precision." Violates Rules 4 + 11.
- Trust strip leading with "★★★★★ 4.9 from 200 reviews" and no founder mention. Violates Rules 5 + 10.
- Parallax-scrolling testimonial wall with auto-play motion. Violates Rule 6.
- Body text at 4.4:1 contrast. Violates Rule 7.
- Warranty page that's a wall of legalese with no plain-language summary. Violates Rule 9.
- "Book your appointment in 30 seconds!" with no phone alternative. Violates Rule 12.

## 10. Worked example

**Trigger.** Request: "Design the warranty / craftsmanship-guarantee page."

**Step 1 — recognize.** Legal/warranty + likely-read-by-grandfather → this doc primary; `ux-design/1.3.1` for shell; `brand-identity/1.2.1` for register; `LEGAL_TRUST_GUIDE.md` playbook for legal substance.

**Step 2 — read source.** Parse PDF. Read trust section + copy register + accessibility section.

**Step 3 — apply rules.** Rule 9: plain-language summary at top, full text below. Rule 1: 18px+ body. Rule 7: ≥ 7:1 contrast. Rule 5: longevity signals (license duration, years in trade, founder name) in the page sidebar. Rule 8: hand-shake language in the summary. Rule 3: phone visible.

**Step 4 — decision.** Page structure: H1 → 2-paragraph plain summary in hand-shake voice → "what this means in practice" bullets → full warranty text → founder signature + license # + years in trade → phone CTA. Body 18px, contrast verified, motion minimal.

**Step 5 — verify.** Plan §12 lists `gr-legal-pages-bespoke`, `gr-wcag-aa`, `gr-real-business-signals`, `gr-motion-system-pinned`.
