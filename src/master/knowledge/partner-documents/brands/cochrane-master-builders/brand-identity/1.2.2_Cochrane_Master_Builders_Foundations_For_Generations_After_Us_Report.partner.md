## 8. Rules — non-negotiables this doc enforces

1. **One tagline per brand surface.** A page never carries two competing taglines (master + remix). Master in footer / about; remix in hero. (source: hero pattern)
2. **Hero promise follows the cadence rules.** Length, rhythm, syllable count per the source. Promises that "fit" but break cadence fail. (source: cadence section)
3. **Sub-tagline elaborates, never duplicates.** Sub-head expands a noun in the tagline; never restates the tagline in different words. (source: hero pattern)
4. **No greeting-card phrasing.** "Building dreams that last forever" and similar collapse the brand into cliché. The source's anti-pattern list governs this. (source: anti-patterns)
5. **Generational claims need a generation behind them.** "For the generations after us" works because it points forward — claiming "four generations of builders" requires four real generations. (cross-link to `1.2.1` Rule 2)
6. **CTA microcopy at brand level is verb-led and singular.** "Start your build" beats "Learn more about getting started today." (source: hero CTA pattern)
7. **Trade-remix taglines derive from the master tagline.** Use the source's candidate alternates as starting point; never freelance an unrelated tagline. (source: candidate alternates)
8. **Mission statements are paragraphs, not slogans.** Never paste the tagline into a mission slot. (source: mission articulation)
9. **Power words for `brand-identity-northstar.ts` come from the source's sanctioned list.** Never harvest from generic copywriting blogs.
10. **Cadence check is mandatory.** Before any hero copy ships, score it against the §11 prompt #4 ("does it pass the cadence rules?"). A fail → revise.

## 9. Anti-patterns

- Hero `Building Dreams. Building Trust. Building Legacies.` — violates Rules 2 + 4 (greeting-card cadence).
- Hero `Custom Homes` + sub-head `We Build Custom Homes` — violates Rule 3 (duplication).
- Drywall remix tagline `Drywall So Smooth You'll Cry` — violates Rule 7 (unrelated to master) + `1.2.1` Rule 1 (startup tone).
- About-page mission slot pasted with the tagline only — violates Rule 8.
- Hero CTA "Click here to learn more about our exclusive limited-time offer" — violates Rule 6.
- "Five generations of master builders" on a 2-year-old remix — violates Rule 5.

## 10. Worked example

**Trigger.** Request: "Write the hero H1 + sub + CTA for the home page."

**Step 1 — recognize.** Hero headline + tagline + CTA microcopy → this doc primary; `seo-research/1.1` for keyword skeleton; `brand-identity/1.2.1` for register; `ux-design/1.3.1` for composition.

**Step 2 — read source.** Parse PDF. Read central tagline + hero pattern + cadence rules.

**Step 3 — apply rules.** Rule 1: master tagline in footer, hero gets a derived promise. Rule 2: cadence-compliant length. Rule 3: sub-head elaborates, never duplicates. Rule 6: verb-led singular CTA. SEO Rule 1 (from `seo-research/1.1`): primary keyword in H1.

**Step 4 — decision.** H1 example shape: short benefit-led promise containing the primary keyword. Sub-head: 14–22 words elaborating one noun from the H1, naming city + warranty signal. CTA: 2–4 word verb-led action. Run §11 prompt #4 (cadence check) before finalizing.

**Step 5 — verify.** Plan §12 lists `gr-bespoke-brand-derivation` (derived from master tagline), `gr-page-meta-jsonld-unique` (H1 unique), `gr-anti-paraphrase-readability` (cadence + readability bands).
