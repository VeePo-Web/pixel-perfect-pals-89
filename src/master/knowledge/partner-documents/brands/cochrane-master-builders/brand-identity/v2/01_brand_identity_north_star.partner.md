## 2. Non-Negotiables / Flex / Dealbreakers

**Non-negotiables (locked):**

- Family-owned identity surfaced on every page.
- Pricing as ranges + drivers + assumptions.
- Master line present at least once per page.
- Cluster mega-menu (never flat 115 links).
- Mobile CTA above fold; sticky mobile bar (Call + Estimate) calm, not flashing.
- No autoplay video.
- No fake claims, fake reviews, fake awards, fake project counts.
- Lighthouse + axe gates (see `11_…`) must pass before publish.

**Allowed flex (per cluster):**

- Hero photography (real material evidence per cluster).
- Accent palette overlay from §9 cheat-sheet.
- One or two bespoke components per service (Weather Protection Scorecard, Waterproofing Explainer, Base Prep Diagram, Outdoor Living Planner, etc.).
- Per-cluster CTA wording from §9.
- Per-community language in Areas We Serve.

**Dealbreakers (immediate fail):**

- Stock family imagery, smiling-contractor stock photos, AI-generated faces.
- Flat 115-service link list anywhere.
- "Best in Cochrane" / "#1 Builder" / unverifiable superlatives.
- Bouncy / parallax / scroll-hijack motion.
- Variable construction pricing presented as fixed.
- Borrowed copy or palette from any spin-off competitor or any reference under `_external-references/`.

## 4. Positioning & Category Ownership

**Positioning statement.** For Cochrane-area homeowners and small-commercial operators who need to trust *before* they hire, Cochrane Master Builders is the family-owned construction services network that turns confusing trades work into a clear, priced, sequenced plan — because durability is built in the work no one sees, and that work deserves to be explained.

**Category we own:** *Family-owned, clarity-first construction services network for Cochrane and the surrounding communities.*

**Unique mechanisms (5):**

1. Cluster-organized service ecosystem (9 clusters, 115 domains, one master family).
2. Pricing-as-ranges-with-drivers (never fixed-price marketing).
3. Hidden-Work explainer pattern on every service page.
4. Per-community geographic specificity (`communities_master_v3` join).
5. Honest proof-building framework when full proof isn't yet available.

**Differentiators vs typical Cochrane contractor sites:**

- We name the hidden work. They show finished photos.
- We show price *ranges* with drivers. They hide price.
- We organize 100+ services in clusters with a master brand. They list services flat.
- We honor the local community name. They keyword-stuff "Cochrane services."
- We refuse fake urgency. They run "limited-time" banners.
- We let process be a trust asset. They treat process as filler.
- We never autoplay video. They open with a 1080p hero loop.
- We show proof or an honest framework — never invented projects.

**Proof architecture (must be visible on the master site):**

- Family-owned standard block on homepage.
- Pricing ranges with drivers above the contact form.
- Process timeline on homepage and every service page.
- Per-community names with real local context in Areas We Serve.
- BBB / municipal / association references **only when verifiable**.

## 6. Customer Identity Mirror

**You are our people if…**

- You're planning a project that has to last decades, not months.
- You'd rather understand the work than be sold to.
- You want a price range with the drivers explained, not a hidden quote.
- You care that the same family stands behind the work after the invoice clears.
- You'd rather wait for the right team than hire whoever calls back first.
- You live in or near Cochrane and value local accountability.
- You'd rather see a process timeline than a stock smiling-family photo.

**Not for you if…**

- You're shopping the lowest bid only.
- You want a same-day verbal price by phone.
- You expect "limited-time" promo discounts.
- You want the cheapest material on every line.

**Customer language bank.**

| Bucket | Phrases (use) |
|--------|---------------|
| Desires | "built to last," "do it once and properly," "know what we're getting into," "long-term value," "no surprises" |
| Fears | "hidden conditions," "permit problems," "blown budget," "leaks years later," "contractor disappears after the invoice" |
| Status / identity | "our home," "our family," "our acreage," "the property we'll live in for years" |
| Objections | "can you actually price this without seeing it?," "what if you find something behind the wall?," "how long will my home be torn up?" |

## 7. Brand Story System

**Thesis:** *Construction is a long story. We make it easier to understand before you commit.*

**Narrative spine:** Confusion → Quiet Doubt → Clear Conversation → Sequenced Plan → Built Right → Long-Term Trust.

**Repeatable themes:**

1. *The Hidden Work.* What lasts is what you can't see.
2. *The Family Standard.* The same name on the truck is the same name on the warranty.
3. *Clear Pricing Is Respect.* Ranges + drivers, not hidden quotes.
4. *Local Reality.* Cochrane wind, freeze-thaw, acreage logistics.
5. *Built To Outlast Us.* Strong foundations for those who come after us.

**Signature moments to dramatize:**

- The first message → first conversation → first walkthrough.
- The "we found something behind the wall" moment, handled openly.
- The final walkthrough hand-off.

**Tagline territory:**

- Strong Foundations For Those Who Come After Us. *(master line — locked)*
- Built Like It Still Matters 50 Years From Now.
- Old-School Accountability. Modern-Day Clarity.
- A Clear Process From First Message To Final Walkthrough.
- Built Like Your Family Will Live With The Result.
- Family-Owned. Locally Built. Long-Term Standard.

## 8. Messaging Pillars + Offer Architecture

**Pillar 1 — Family-Owned Accountability.** Proof: same family answers, runs, signs off. CTA verbs: *Talk through, Walk us through, Tell us what you know.*

**Pillar 2 — Clarity Before Cost.** Proof: pricing ranges + drivers + assumptions. CTA verbs: *Request a clear estimate, Send what you know, Plan your project.*

**Pillar 3 — Hidden-Work Standard.** Proof: Materials & Methods sections, process timeline. CTA verbs: *Request a site scope review, Get a first look, Talk through scope.*

**Pillar 4 — Local Reality.** Proof: per-community language, freeze-thaw / wind / acreage notes. CTA verbs: *See areas served, Tell us where, Send your address.*

**Pillar 5 — Built To Outlast Us.** Proof: master legacy block, family standard applied, long-term warranty language only when verifiable. CTA verbs: *Build it properly, Plan for the long term, Start the conversation.*

(Each pillar's 10 headlines / 10 subheads / 10 CTAs are authored in `09_voice_messaging_lexicon.partner.md`.)

**Offer architecture per service page:**

1. Fast Answer (50–90 words).
2. Services Offered (specific sub-services + price ranges + outcomes).
3. Pricing tiers — Small / Standard / Premium-Complex — with drivers.
4. Process — universal steps + service-specific steps.
5. Final CTA matched to service emotion.

## 9. Verbal Identity (Voice / Tone / Lexicon)

2. Does it explain the price?

## 12. What Not To Do (hard guardrails)

- Don't claim premium without a proof mechanic.
- Don't list services flat — always cluster.
- Don't show stock family imagery.
- Don't promise fixed prices on variable construction.
- Don't fabricate projects, reviews, or awards.
- Don't autoplay video.
- Don't ship a page that fails the perf budget.
- Don't borrow tokens, copy, or values from any external reference.
