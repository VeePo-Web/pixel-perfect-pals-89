## Main Purpose
This document establishes the exact psychological profile and UX expectations for **The Grandfather / The Patriarch**. This persona represents the older, highly traditional, no-nonsense decision maker who values generational longevity, "handshake trust," and elite craftsmanship over flashy, modern trends. In the context of Master Builders, this persona ensures the website retains a deeply anchored, heritage-brand feel (similar to luxury watch brands or heritage automotive).

## What This Document Influences
This document acts as an immediate backend decision filter for:
- **Heritage & Legacy Copywriting:** It shapes the "Our Story," "Our Legacy," and "About Us" sections to sound authoritative, historically rooted, and completely devoid of "modern corporate jargon." 
- **UX Legibility & Navigation:** Dictates that while the UX must be `fantasy.co` quality, it cannot be abstract or overly "techy/confusing." Navigation must be brutally intuitive, typography must be highly legible, and contrast must be perfect.
- **Trust Architecture:** Focuses the layout heavily on proof of durability, longevity of the business, and absolute elite craftsmanship. It appeals to the desire to "build something that lasts for generations."
- **Sub-Brand Generation:** When expanding to child services (e.g., Cochrane Tile Masters), the copywriting must respect the history of the trade and the mastery of the craft.

## When Should the System Consult This?
Code assistants and AI systems must consult this document whenever asked to:
1. Write the "About Us," "History," or "Our Craftsmanship" sections.
2. Dial in the typography scale, focusing on readability and authoritative serif/sans-serif pairings.
3. Design the overarching navigation hierarchy to ensure it isn't overly abstract or hidden behind complex gestures.

**Practical Example:** If the user asks "Design the About Us page layout," the AI must consult this document to ensure the page feels like an old-world luxury brand (heavy on trust, durability, and generational quality) rather than a fast-moving, disposable startup.
