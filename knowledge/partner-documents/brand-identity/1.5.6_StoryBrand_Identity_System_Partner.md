# Partner Document: 1.5.6 — StoryBrand Identity System

## What This Document Is

This is the **narrative engine** for the Cochrane Master Builders brand. It does not govern aesthetics or SEO — it governs the *emotional logic* of every word written on every page. It is built on Donald Miller's StoryBrand 7-part framework, applied to a generational luxury construction brand.

The north-star slogan that anchors the entire system:

> **"Building strong foundations for those who come after us."**

This document contains:
- The complete 7-part StoryBrand narrative script (verbatim, ready to deploy)
- An approved copy library: 20+ headline variants, 10 body paragraphs, approved CTA language
- Tone rules: what the brand sounds like and what it never sounds like
- Cross-site transferability protocol: how every VeePo sub-brand inherits and adapts this system
- Banned words and banned patterns

## The 7-Part StoryBrand Narrative Script

This is the master brand story. Every page, every section, every line of copy must be traceable to one of these seven stages.

---

### Stage 1 — The Character (The Hero)

The hero is not the company. The hero is the **homeowner** — specifically:

**The Matriarch:** She is the primary decision-maker for the home. She needs the work done right the first time. Mess, uncertainty, and contractors who disappear are her biggest fears. She values clarity, cleanliness, and a professional who treats her home with the same care she does.

**The Patriarch:** He is the legacy thinker. He has been around long enough to know the difference between a contractor who knows his trade and one who is filling space. He buys craftsmanship, not price. He wants to pass down a home that reflects his values.

**Copy to use:**
- "Your home is the most important thing you will ever pass down."
- "The matriarch who needs the work done right the first time."
- "The patriarch who recognizes craftsmanship when he sees it."
- "People who understand that a wall is not just a wall."

---

### Stage 2 — The Problem

**External problem (visible):** There is damage, incomplete work, or a construction need that requires a skilled contractor.

**Internal problem (emotional):** The homeowner has been burned before. They hired someone who did it badly, or who disappeared, or who left the space worse than they found it. They feel let down and are now skeptical.

**Philosophical problem (worldview):** In a world of fast, cheap, disposable work — there are almost no contractors who build to last. This is wrong. Legacy homes deserve legacy craftsmanship.

**Copy to use:**
- "Most contractors aren't built for work that lasts. They're built for speed."
- "The job they call too small to bother with — until you hire someone who does it badly."
- "The contractor who gives a quote, then disappears for six weeks."
- "The wall you have been looking at for a year because it never felt like the right time."
- "Small damage becomes structural compromise when you wait."

---

### Stage 3 — The Guide (Cochrane Master Builders)

The guide must demonstrate two things: **empathy** (we understand the problem) and **authority** (we have solved it for many people like you).

**Empathy signals:**
- "We understand what it feels like to be let down by a contractor."
- "We take that trust seriously — because we've been doing this long enough to know what happens when someone doesn't."

**Authority signals:**
- Fourth-generation craftsmanship
- 15-year structural warranty
- Cochrane born and raised
- Project completion guarantee in writing

**Copy to use:**
- "We do not build projects. We build the parts of your home your grandchildren will touch."
- "Four generations of Cochrane craftsmen. Every project carries that name."
- "We have been in this community long enough to know that our reputation is the only thing that matters."

---

### Stage 4 — The Plan

The plan must eliminate hesitation. It must feel simple, clear, and risk-free.

**Three-step simplified version (for heroes, CTAs):**
1. Send photos — two pictures and a sentence
2. Receive a transparent range — in writing, within one business day
3. Get the work done right — guaranteed

**Four-step detailed version (for process pages):**
1. **Send Your Photos** — Two photographs and one sentence. No phone calls, no commitment.
2. **Receive a Transparent Range** — Within one business day. A number you can actually plan around.
3. **A Contained, Respected Worksite** — We arrive on time. We protect your home as if it were ours.
4. **The Generational Finish** — We leave when the result is finished. Not patched. Finished.

**Copy to use:**
- "A process built for people who have been let down before."
- "Within one business day — a realistic ballpark tied to your specific scope, in writing."
- "We arrive on time, confine the work area, protect your home as if it were ours."
- "Touch-ups guaranteed in writing for 14 days. Structural work for 15 years."

---

### Stage 5 — The Call to Action

CTAs must never feel desperate or generic. They must feel like an invitation from a trusted authority.

**Primary CTA (always):** `Get Your Free Estimate`
**Secondary CTA:** `View Our Work` or `See Our Process`
**Tertiary trust anchors (inline, small caps):** `Free Estimate · Cochrane Team · Guaranteed in Writing`

**Never use:**
- "Get a Quote" (too transactional)
- "Book Now" (too urgent, not premium)
- "Contact Us" (completely generic)
- "Learn More" (zero commitment from the brand)
- "Sign Up" (SaaS language — never)

**Approved variants of the primary CTA:**
- "Get Your Free Estimate"
- "Schedule Your Consultation"
- "Begin Your Project"
- "Request Your Assessment"

---

### Stage 6 — Failure (What Happens Without Action)

Always present failure as a consequence of inaction — never as a judgment of the homeowner. This creates urgency without pressure.

**Copy to use:**
- "Small damage becomes structural compromise."
- "The contractor you waited to call charges double — if they even return your call."
- "The home you pass down carries the weight of what you chose not to do."
- "The wall does not fix itself."
- "Every season you delay is a season the problem grows."

---

### Stage 7 — Success (The Transformation)

Success is not a completed project. Success is **legacy preserved**.

**Copy to use:**
- "Work done once, done completely, with a guarantee attached."
- "A clean site, clear communication, and a result you walk past with pride."
- "A foundation that outlasts the reason you needed it fixed."
- "Your foundation. Their inheritance."
- "Every great family home started with a single decision to build it right."
- "The room you walk past, finally finished."
- "A home built to outlast the hands that built it."

## Tone Rules

### The brand sounds like:
- Permanent — statements that will be true in 50 years
- Authoritative — confident, never boastful
- Warm — like a trusted craftsman explaining his work to a homeowner, not a sales pitch
- Specific — concrete details, not vague promises
- Legacy-conscious — always aware that the work will outlast the people in the room

### The brand never sounds like:
- Urgent or desperate ("Call now!")
- Generic SaaS ("Seamless experience", "Next-level results")
- Cheap or discount-focused ("Affordable rates!")
- Hyperbolic ("World-class service")
- Vague ("Quality work at competitive prices")

### Banned words (never appear in copy):
| Banned | Use Instead |
|--------|-------------|
| Quick | Efficient |
| Cheap / Affordable | Transparent pricing / Value-driven |
| Fast | Predictable timeline |
| Modern | Precision / Craft |
| Seamless | Contained / Clean |
| Hassle-free | Straightforward |
| Best-in-class | Generational standard |
| Unleash | — (never) |
| Elevate | — (never) |
| Game-changer | — (never) |

## Cross-Site Transferability Protocol

Every VeePo Master Builder sub-website (Cochrane Tile Masters, Drywall Masters, etc.) inherits this system with three adaptations:

### 1. serviceName swap
Replace "Cochrane Master Builders" → "[Trade] Masters" throughout  
The `<GenerationalBadge serviceName="Tile Masters" />` component handles this automatically.

### 2. tradeDescriptor swap
Replace "construction" / "drywall" with the precise trade term:
- Tile Masters: "tile & stone work"
- Drywall Masters: "walls & ceilings"
- Insulation Masters: "thermal protection"

### 3. Trade-specific authority pillars
Replace the three authority pillars in §4 with trade-specific proof:
- Tile Masters: "4,000+ sq ft laid" / "100% lippage-free guarantee" / "Cochrane-based tile specialists"
- Drywall Masters: "Level 5 finish standard" / "Dust-contained worksite" / "14-day touch-up guarantee"

**Everything else — the 7 StoryBrand stages, the tone rules, the CTA hierarchy, the north-star slogan — remains identical across every sub-site.**
