# Partner Document: 1.5.7 — Trade Manifesto Template System

## What This Document Is

This is the **passion layer** of the Cochrane Master Builders brand identity. While the StoryBrand system (1.5.6) governs the rational narrative arc — character, problem, guide, plan — the Trade Manifesto governs the **emotional obsession signal**: the content that makes a visitor think *"these people are absolutely unhinged about this trade, and I have never felt more confident hiring them."*

The system is built as a typed `ManifestoConfig` object (`src/config/manifesto/manifesto.types.ts`) that any sub-brand fills in to instantly generate their full `/why-we-love` page via the `<TradeManifesto>` component. Zero new component code needed per trade — fill the config, ship the page.

**Slogan / north-star:** *"Building strong foundations for those who come after us."*  
**Page URL:** `/why-we-love` (standardized across all 150 sites)

## The ManifestoConfig Fill-in Template

To create a "Why We Love [X]" manifesto for any trade, fill in the following fields. Reference `drywall.manifesto.ts` as the gold standard example.

```typescript
// src/config/manifesto/[trade].manifesto.ts

import type { ManifestoConfig } from "./manifesto.types";
// Import appropriate atmospheric images for your trade

export const [trade]Manifesto: ManifestoConfig = {
  tradeName: "[TRADE NAME — Title Case, e.g. 'Tile']",
  tradeNounLower: "[trade name lowercase, e.g. 'tile']",
  tradeNounPlural: "[what the trade produces, e.g. 'tile floors and backsplashes']",
  serviceFullName: "Cochrane [Trade] Masters",
  pageTitle: "Why We Love [Trade] — Cochrane [Trade] Masters",
  subtitle: "[A confession from people who... — keep it dry, specific, slightly funny]",

  hook: {
    casualView: "[1-2 sentences: what a normal person sees. Should sound almost dismissive — 'just a floor' etc.]",
    obsessiveView: "[3-5 sentences: what YOU see. Must include specific technical details the average person would never notice. No abstractions.]",
    giftStatement: "[The pivot. Keep it short. 'This is either a problem or a gift. We decided it is a gift.']",
    benefitStatement: "[One sentence. 'Your [X] is benefiting from this decision.']",
  },

  escalation: {
    unknownKnowledgeItems: [
      "[Thing 1 avg person doesn't know — be technical and specific]",
      "[Thing 2 — make this one a bit longer, with a parenthetical that reveals depth]",
      "[Thing 3 — this one should be funny in its specificity]",
    ],
    brandClaim: "[The 'we are not the average homeowner' statement. Should include one funny specific admission.]",
    specificFact: "[THE FUNNY MOMENT — THE CALLOUT BOX. One paragraph. This is the line people screenshot. Must reveal the obsession at its most specific and slightly unhinged. Should make the reader laugh but simultaneously trust the brand completely.]",
  },

  originStory: {
    paragraph: "[Specific story: a specific job, specific detail, specific year if possible. 3-5 sentences. True or archetypally true. Shows the obsession in action — the thing they refused to shortcut.]",
    punchline: "[The generational kicker. Shows the long view. Usually involves a child, a future owner, or a future craftsman who will someday encounter the quality.]",
  },

  whyItMatters: "[One strong paragraph. Pivots from 'here is our obsession' to 'here is what you get because of it.' Uses the specific material as a metaphor for legacy. No abstractions. Ends on the customer, not the craftsman.]",

  close: {
    awarenessLine: "[We are aware this is a significant amount of feeling about [tradeNounPlural].]",
    noChangeStatement: "[Short. 'We do not plan to change.' Or a variant.]",
    alternativeAcknowledgment: "[One sentence acknowledging the faster/cheaper option. Confident, not defensive.]",
    finalLine: "[THE KICKER. The last line of the entire page. Use \\n for the two-line punchline effect. This is the line that must be perfect. See examples below.]",
  },

  heroImage: [importedImage],
  atmosphericImage: [importedImage],  // optional
};
```

## Tone Guidelines — Field by Field

### `hook.casualView`
**DO:** Sound like the customer in their own head. Neutral, practical, even slightly bored. This is what normal people think.  
**DON'T:** Be dismissive or condescending toward the customer. The contrast is gentle.  
**Example:** "Most people see a bathroom floor. They see a surface that is either clean or not clean."

### `hook.obsessiveView`
**DO:** Stack specific technical observations one after another. The accumulation is the effect. At least 3 specifics.  
**DON'T:** Use abstractions like "quality" or "craftsmanship" without immediately following them with a concrete example.  
**The test:** Could a competitor copy this paragraph? If yes, it's not specific enough.

### `escalation.specificFact` (THE CALLOUT BOX)
**This is the most important field.** It must:
- Start with a factual claim ("Here is an objectively true thing:", "Here is what nobody tells you:")
- Contain a specific number, percentage, or measurement
- Reveal something absurd but completely believable about the craft
- Explain the obsession (why they take longer, why they care more)
- End with a small self-aware admission

**The test:** Read it aloud. If it makes you laugh and then immediately trust them more, it's right.

### `originStory.paragraph`
**DO:** Use a specific year. A specific location ("in Cochrane," "on the south end of town"). A specific detail (sq ft, days, the exact thing they refused to shortcut). Make it feel real.  
**DON'T:** Generalize. "We once worked on a project..." is too vague. "In 2019, we were finishing a basement stairwell in Cochrane..." is correct.

### `close.finalLine`
**This is the hardest line to write.** Rules:
- Use `\n` to create the two-line effect (first line sets up, second line is the kicker)
- The second line must be shorter than the first
- It must reference something specific from the story or the trade
- It should sound slightly like a challenge or an invitation, not a plea
- **Test:** Is this the line they screenshot? If not, rewrite it.

**Bad:** "We take pride in every job.\nCall us today."  
**Good:** "We will still be on your stairwell ceiling for eleven days.\nCome find us when that matters."

### Banned words in manifesto copy:
| Banned | Why |
|--------|-----|
| "Seamless" | Generic SaaS language |
| "Passionate" | Never say it — show it |
| "Quality" | Too abstract |
| "Excellence" | Sounds corporate |
| "We pride ourselves" | Cliché |
| "Industry-leading" | Meaningless |
| "Solutions" | Never |
| "Affordable" | Wrong brand |

## Example: Tile Masters — Complete Filled Manifesto

*A second complete example to guide writers for any new trade.*

**tradeName:** `"Tile"`  
**subtitle:** `"A note from people who will never stop thinking about lippage"`

---

### hook.casualView
"Most people see a bathroom floor. They see a surface that is either clean or not clean, done or not done, and that is the complete extent of their relationship with it."

### hook.obsessiveView
"We see a 1.2mm lippage on the third row from the wall — the tile edge that rides higher than its neighbor by less than the thickness of a credit card, invisibly except to someone who runs their fingertip across it and knows exactly what they are feeling. We see a grout joint that is 2mm wide on one side of the room and 2.5mm wide on the other, a variance that no homeowner will ever measure but that every homeowner will perceive as some inexplicable unease about the space. We see a diagonal layout that was not properly centered on the room's optical midpoint, so the cut tiles at the far wall are thirteen millimetres narrower than the cut tiles at the near wall — and nobody will ever be able to explain why the room feels slightly wrong, but it will feel slightly wrong forever."

### hook.giftStatement
"This is either a problem or a gift. We decided it is a gift."

### hook.benefitStatement
"Your floors are benefiting from this decision."

---

### escalation.unknownKnowledgeItems
1. `"what tile lippage actually means — or that 1/32 inch is the industry maximum and that most residential installations exceed it by a factor of three"`
2. `"the difference between medium-bed mortar and large-format tile mortar, or why using the wrong one on a 24×24 tile causes hollow spots that won't announce themselves until year four"`
3. `"that the dry layout — the four hours before the first tile goes down — is the most important step in any tile installation, and that most contractors skip it entirely"`

### escalation.brandClaim
"We are not most contractors. We are the people who have measured a grout joint in someone else's bathroom at a dinner party — with a pocket gauge we carry — and said nothing, but felt something. We cannot help it."

### escalation.specificFact
"Here is the truth about tile layout: the most important decision in any tile installation happens before the first tile goes down. It is called the dry layout. It takes four hours. It moves no tiles permanently. It produces nothing a client can photograph or a contractor can bill for. Most contractors skip it entirely. We have never, in the history of our operation, skipped the dry layout. We have been gently mocked for this. We have also never had a room feel slightly wrong."

---

### originStory.paragraph
"In 2021, we were tiling a master bathroom in Cochrane — a herringbone layout in a long, narrow room with a window that sat slightly off-centre of the vanity wall. The standard approach would have been to centre the layout on the door. We centred it on the window instead, which required recalculating the entire layout grid from scratch and re-cutting the border tiles. It added six hours. The homeowner never noticed the window alignment. They noticed, and commented on, the fact that the room felt balanced."

### originStory.punchline
"That bathroom was photographed for a renovation feature three months later. The photographer asked us what we had done to make the proportions 'feel so right.' We explained the dry layout and the window centering. He looked at us for a moment and then said he was going to take the photo anyway. We took that as a compliment."

---

### whyItMatters
"When someone carries a pocket gauge to dinner parties, you do not get a floor that looks fine on installation day and starts telegraphing its compromises by year three — the grout that cracks, the hollow tiles that shift, the layout that nobody can diagnose as wrong but that everyone registers as off. You get a floor that is correct in the ways that matter before you can see them. You get the foundation under every morning of your family's life in that room."

---

### close.awarenessLine
"We are aware this is a significant amount of feeling about ceramic and stone."

### close.noChangeStatement
"We do not plan to change."

### close.alternativeAcknowledgment
"If you want someone who skips the dry layout and finishes in two days, that contractor exists and is easy to find."

### close.finalLine
`"The grout will be perfect.\nOr we will be back until it is."`
