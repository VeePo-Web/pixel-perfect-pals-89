# Partner Document: 1.5.5 Volume 6 — Conversion, CRM, Proof & Governance System

## What This Document Is

Volume 6 is the operational conversion engine for the Cochrane Master Builders brand. It does not address aesthetics or SEO — it governs everything that happens **after** a visitor lands on the site: how they are captured, qualified, nurtured, converted into a paying client, and then transformed into a proof asset for future conversions.

This is a 55-page system covering 27 interconnected subsystems: CTA hierarchy, form architecture, lead qualification, CRM pipeline (15 stages), follow-up cadences, sales conversation scripts, proposal governance, project communication standards, review generation, proof library management, analytics governance, claims handling, reputation recovery, lifecycle nurture, and a complete implementation roadmap.

## Output Goals When Consulting This Document

### 1. CTA Architecture
All calls-to-action must follow the three-tier hierarchy defined in this document: **Primary CTA** (Get Your Free Estimate), **Secondary CTA** (View Our Work / See Our Process), **Tertiary CTA** (Trust anchors, guarantee statements). Never invent CTA copy outside this hierarchy. Button friction, placement, and color must reinforce the luxury brand while eliminating hesitation.

### 2. Form Architecture
All intake/quote forms must use the exact field structure and friction-reduction logic specified here. Progressive disclosure (2–3 fields initially, expand after micro-commitment) is mandatory. No generic "Contact Us" form patterns. Every field must have a stated purpose that reinforces trust.

### 3. Lead Qualification & CRM
The 15-stage CRM pipeline is the authoritative structure for all lead routing decisions. Any automation, webhook, or CRM integration must map to these stages. Do not collapse or invent stages. The qualification script and scoring criteria defined here determine what constitutes a qualified lead vs. a disqualified inquiry.

### 4. Follow-Up & Sales Conversations
All follow-up email and SMS templates, cadence timing, and sales conversation frameworks must be drawn from or remain consistent with this document. Response time SLAs (e.g., <2 hour first response) are binding standards.

### 5. Proposal & Estimate Governance
Proposal structure, section order, pricing presentation logic, and the approval/revision workflow are all specified. Any proposal or estimate UI must enforce these standards. The "Investment Summary" framing (never "Quote" or "Price") is mandatory.

### 6. Proof, Reviews & Reputation
The review generation sequence, proof collection triggers (post-project milestone), Project Story System format, and Proof Library taxonomy are all authoritative. Any testimonial section, case study page, or review widget must be structured to match this system. The reputation risk / recovery protocol governs how negative reviews are handled.

### 7. Analytics & Governance
KPI definitions, dashboard structure, weekly/monthly review cadence, and governance decision thresholds are defined here. Any analytics integration or reporting feature must align to these metrics.

---

## Critical Rules

- **Do NOT** use generic SaaS-style CTA copy ("Sign Up," "Get Started," "Learn More") — consult the CTA hierarchy in this document for all button text.
- **Do NOT** build a single long form — progressive disclosure and micro-commitment architecture are required.
- **Do NOT** collapse the 15 CRM pipeline stages — each stage has distinct automation and communication logic.
- **Do NOT** present pricing as a "Quote" — it must be framed as an "Investment Summary" within a formal proposal structure.
- **Do NOT** solicit reviews without following the post-project proof collection trigger sequence defined here.
