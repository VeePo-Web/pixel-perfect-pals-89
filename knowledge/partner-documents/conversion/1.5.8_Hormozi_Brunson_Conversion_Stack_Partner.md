# Partner Document: 1.5.8 — The Hormozi + Brunson Conversion Stack

## What This Document Is

This is the **conversion intelligence layer** for the Cochrane Master Builders brand. It translates Alex Hormozi's $100M Offers framework and Russell Brunson's DotCom/Expert Secrets framework into specific, actionable copy, component rules, and page structures for the construction services context.

**The core problem it solves:** A brand can be beautiful and emotionally resonant — and still fail to convert visitors into booked clients. This document governs everything between "visitor arrives" and "client signs."

## PART 1: Alex Hormozi — $100M Offers Framework Applied to Construction

### The Grand Slam Offer (GSO)

A Grand Slam Offer is a NAMED, PACKAGED offer so specific and de-risked that the customer feels stupid saying no. It is not a price. It is not a quote. It is a package.

**The construction GSO formula:**
`[Trade] + [Standard] + [Process guarantee] + [Result guarantee] + [Risk elimination]`

Example: *"The Generational Finish Guarantee — Level 5 standard, contained worksite, written scope, 14-day touch-up, 15-year structural warranty. All included. In writing."*

**Why it works:** The customer's decision moves from "can I trust this?" to "can I afford NOT to?" The risk is fully transferred to the contractor.

### The Value Equation

`Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)`

**Application:**

| Lever | Construction application |
|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | A home that looks finished, lasts for generations, reflects intentional ownership |
| Perceived Likelihood | Specific testimonials with outcomes + numbered guarantee + track record in the community |
| Time Delay | Same-day photo response, predictable project window, no spiral |
| Effort & Sacrifice | One photo text, one written quote, single point of contact, no multi-meeting process |

**Every page element must move at least one of these four levers. If it doesn't, cut it.**

### The 3-Tier Risk Reversal Guarantee

Hormozi: "The guarantee should scare you a little. If it doesn't, it's not strong enough."

**Tier 1 — The Worksite Guarantee:**
"If our site is not visibly cleaner than we found it when we arrived, the work is free."
*Why it works: signals discipline and respect before the work even starts.*

**Tier 2 — The Touch-Up Guarantee:**
"Any finish issue within 14 days: we return at zero cost. No argument. No second invoice."
*Why it works: eliminates the #1 contractor fear — being left with a bad result and no recourse.*

**Tier 3 — The Structural Guarantee:**
"Any structural work we complete is warranted for 15 years in writing on every invoice."
*Why it works: industry standard is 1–2 years. 15 years in writing signals an entirely different class of contractor.*

**Critical rules:**
- All three must be in writing on every invoice (not just on the website)
- Display them everywhere: hero sections, service pages, contact page, the offer page
- Never dilute them with exceptions or fine print on the marketing surfaces

### Who This Is For / Who This Is NOT For

**Purpose:** Pre-qualifies leads (saves time) AND converts the right customers by making them feel seen.

**Who This Is For:**
"Homeowners who have been burned before. Who know the difference between a contractor who cares and one who just wants to move to the next job. Who understand that paying once for something done right is not the same as paying twice for something done fast."

**Who This Is NOT For:**
"If you need the cheapest option available, we are not it. If your timeline is 'yesterday' regardless of quality, we are not the right fit. We will tell you this directly, and you can find a contractor who will serve you better in that context."

*The "Not For" creates the "For" customers to feel MORE committed — not less.*

### Testimonial Structure (Before/After Results Format)

**Weak (never use):** "Great work, very professional. Would recommend."
**Strong (always use):**
- Before state: "Had a crack running down the hallway wall for two years."
- After state: "They patched it properly. You genuinely cannot find it."
- Timeframe: "In and out in an afternoon."
- Specific detail: "Charged exactly what they quoted."

**Rule:** Every testimonial must answer: What was wrong? What happened? How long did it take? What was the result?

### Price Anchoring

Always show what the alternative costs BEFORE showing your price.

**Template:** "A general contractor charges $[HIGH]–$[HIGHER] for [SERVICE]. Our range is $[LOW]–$[MID] for the same scope — because we specialize in exactly this, and we don't bring [EXCESSIVE TRADES] when [EFFICIENT APPROACH] will do."

### Scarcity — Real Capacity Signals

**Never fake urgency.** Real scarcity converts better and never destroys trust.

Acceptable signals:
- "Currently booking [X] weeks out" (update weekly, from `capacity.config.ts`)
- "[N] project slots remaining in [Month]" (update monthly)

Unacceptable: countdown timers that reset, fake "limited spots" claims, artificial urgency.

## PART 2: Russell Brunson — DotCom Secrets + Expert Secrets Framework

### The Value Ladder (Ascension Model)

Every customer needs a clear path from FREE → ENTRY → CORE → PREMIUM.

**Construction Value Ladder:**

| Tier | Offer | Price | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free | Photo assessment guide / "7 Questions to Ask Any Contractor" PDF | $0 | Lead capture, build trust |
| Entry | Small repair visits | $150–$450 | Tripwire — first transaction, builds relationship |
| Core | Installation or room package | $900–$3,500 | Core revenue |
| Premium | Full basement/garage package | $3,500–$8,000+ | Profit maximizer |
| Continuity | Referral / repeat project / warranty work | Ongoing | Return path + advocacy |

**Rule:** Every page should show the visitor where they are on this ladder and what's next. Never leave the customer without a clear "next step."

### Hook / Story / Offer — Every Page Has ONE Job

Brunson's universal page structure:

**Hook:** The one sentence that stops the scroll. Makes them read the next sentence.
- Bad hook: "Cochrane Drywall & Insulation"
- Good hook: "Finally get that wall handled."
- Better hook: "Most contractors call your job too small. We call it exactly the kind of work we do best."

**Story:** Why this is true. The evidence. Not a feature list — a narrative.
- The origin story (the stairwell ceiling)
- The testimonial that addresses their fear
- The specific process that removes their uncertainty

**Offer:** One action. One button. One clear next step. Never two.

**Critical:** Every page should have exactly ONE primary CTA. Remove all competing actions.

### The Three Core Beliefs (and False Beliefs to Break)

Before someone buys a premium construction service, three beliefs must be installed:

**Belief 1: This contractor is different from all the others I've used.**

False belief to break: *"All contractors say they're different."*
Install via: Specific Level 5 standard + guarantee in writing + testimonial of someone who was burned before + origin story of the craftsman who refused shortcuts.

**Belief 2: I won't get ripped off or scope-crept.**

False belief to break: *"Every contractor scope-creeps."*
Install via: Written quote + written scope + nothing-without-approval policy + "The most common contractor complaint we hear from new clients is being surprised by the invoice."

**Belief 3: The quality is worth the price.**

False belief to break: *"Quality construction always costs too much."*
Install via: Price anchor (what inferior alternatives cost) + "paying twice costs more than paying once" framing + 15-year guarantee that eliminates re-payment risk.

### The Attractive Character — The Generational Craftsman

Brunson: people buy FROM a person, not a company. Every Master Builder sub-site needs a human character with these four elements:

**1. Backstory:**
"My grandfather did this. My father did this. I watched both of them refuse to take shortcuts even when shortcuts were the easier option. I didn't fully understand why until I was standing in a stairwell ceiling for three extra days on a job that didn't require it."

**2. Character Flaw (that shows the obsession):**
"I cannot stop noticing other people's drywall in social situations. My family has accepted this about me. My clients benefit from it."

**3. Moral Authority:**
"We have been in this community for forty years. We will be here forty years from now. Our reputation is the only thing we have, and we treat it that way."

**4. Mission (The Cause):**
"There are contractors who build for the invoice. We build for the family that will live in this house for the next thirty years. If you agree that it matters — come build with us."

### The Epiphany Bridge — About Page Structure

The About page must not be a company history. It must be the moment the founder's belief changed.

**Epiphany Bridge Structure:**
1. **Old belief:** "Construction is a job. Do it, get paid, move on."
2. **The Event:** [Specific moment — the job they refused to shortcut, the homeowner who came back 15 years later, the crack they spent three extra days getting right]
3. **New belief:** "This work outlasts me. If I'm going to attach my name to it, it has to be right. It's not about the invoice — it's about what this house will tell people about my family in 30 years."
4. **Result:** "That's why we do what we do. Not because of a marketing strategy. Because of a stairwell ceiling in Cochrane."

### The Cause — "Building for Generations" as Ideology

Brunson: the most powerful brands are movements, not companies.

**The Cause copy:**
"There are contractors who build for the invoice. There are contractors who build for the family that will live in this house for the next thirty years. We are the second kind. If you agree that it matters — if you understand the difference between a wall that looks fine and a wall that lasts — come build with us."

*This is not a tagline. It is an ideology. Display it prominently: footer, About page, the-offer page. Make it the lens through which all other copy is read.*

### Email Soap Opera Sequence (5-Email Post-Booking Framework)

After ANY first contact (photo submission, booking, form fill), send this sequence:

**Email 1 — Day 0 (Booking confirmation):** *The Cause*
Subject: "You chose the right team — here's why that matters"
Content: Brief confirmation + the cause statement + what to expect next.
Tone: Warm, confident, specific. Not generic. Not template.

**Email 2 — Day 1:** *The Origin Story*
Subject: "The stairwell ceiling (and why we're still on it)"
Content: The origin story — the specific job, the specific choice, the generational punchline.
Purpose: Builds the Attractive Character. Makes them trust the PERSON, not just the company.

**Email 3 — Day 3:** *The Value Stack*
Subject: "What's actually included — and what it's worth"
Content: Walk through the Grand Slam Offer items one by one. The 15-year guarantee. The written scope.
Purpose: Reinforces the value before the job starts. Pre-sells the result.

**Email 4 — Day Before Visit:** *What to Expect*
Subject: "Tomorrow — here's exactly what will happen"
Content: Arrival window, site containment process, how decisions will be communicated, what the finished result will look like. Reduce every possible anxiety.
Purpose: The matriarch persona specifically needs this. Reduces call-backs, complaints, uncertainty.

**Email 5 — Post-Project:** *The Ask + The Referral*
Subject: "[First name], would you be willing to do one thing?"
Content: Direct ask for a Google review with a link. Followed by: "If you know someone who has a wall or space that needs the same treatment — we'd be grateful for the referral. We'll take the same care of them."
Purpose: Activates the advocacy flywheel. Triggers Volume 6's review generation sequence.

## PART 3: The Conversion Stack Checklist

Every page on every sub-site should pass this checklist before launch:

### Required on every page:
- [ ] **Hook** — One sentence that stops the scroll
- [ ] **Trust signals** — Specific, not generic ("Level 5 finish standard" not "high quality")
- [ ] **Social proof** — At least one Before/After testimonial with specific outcome
- [ ] **Risk reversal** — At least the compact `<GuaranteeBlock>` visible
- [ ] **Single primary CTA** — One button. No competing calls to action.
- [ ] **The Cause** — The "Building for Generations" ideology visible somewhere

### Required on the homepage:
- [ ] Capacity signal (honest scarcity)
- [ ] Value ladder teaser (at minimum a "where to start" section)

## PART 4: The Conversion Copy Library

### Approved Offer Names
- "The Generational Finish Guarantee" (primary)
- "The Written-Scope Promise"
- "The No-Surprise Project"

### Approved Guarantee Language
- "Guaranteed in writing on every invoice."
- "Not a policy. A promise on paper."
- "15 years. In writing. Non-negotiable."
- "If it's not right in 14 days, we come back. At zero cost."

### Approved Price Anchor Templates
- "A general contractor charges $X–$Y for this scope. Our range: $A–$B — because we specialize in exactly this."
- "Paying twice costs more than paying once. The guarantee eliminates the re-payment risk."
- "The alternative is a $[X] call-back you shouldn't have to make."

### Approved Scarcity Language
- "Currently booking [X] weeks out."
- "[N] project slots remaining in [Month]."
- "We only run [N] active projects at once. This is intentional."

### Approved "Who This Is For" Variants
1. "Homeowners who have been burned before."
2. "Families who understand that paying once for something done right is better than paying twice."
3. "People who will notice the difference between Level 3 and Level 5 — and care."

### Banned Conversion Copy
| Banned | Why |
|--------|-----|
| "Limited time offer" | Fake urgency destroys trust |
| "Act now before it's too late" | Desperate |
| "Best value in town" | Meaningless, unprovable |
| "We're passionate about..." | Tell don't show |
| "Satisfaction guaranteed" | Too vague, too common |
| "Call us today!" | Generic, low-trust |

## PART 5: The Lead Magnet Roadmap

Three lead magnets to build, in priority order:

**1. "The 7 Questions to Ask Any Contractor Before You Hire Them"** (HIGHEST PRIORITY)
- PDF, downloadable via email capture
- Positions CMB as the authority, filters out bad contractors (who CMB isn't)
- CMB answers all 7 questions perfectly (written guarantee, Level 5 standard, written scope, etc.)
- CTA at end: "Want to hire someone who answers all 7 correctly? We do."

**2. "The Homeowner's Photo Assessment Guide"** (MEDIUM PRIORITY)
- PDF or landing page: how to take the right photos to get an accurate quote
- Angles, lighting, what to show, what to include in the description
- Reduces back-and-forth, improves quote accuracy
- Builds trust before first contact

**3. "The Construction Finish Quality Guide: What Level 5 Means and Why It Matters"** (LOWER PRIORITY)
- Installs the "Level 5 is the standard" belief before they ever contact a contractor
- Positions every competitor as offering inferior work by comparison
- Establishes CMB as the expert authority on finish quality
